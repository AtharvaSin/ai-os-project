# Content Tone & Copy Rules — Atharva Singh

> Read this file when generating text content for visuals. The right words in the right voice make branded assets feel intentional, not generated.

---

## Context A — AI OS: Technical Precision

**Voice:** An operating system reporting its own state. Calm, confident, data-driven.

**Headline rules:**
- Sentence case, never ALL CAPS
- Lead with the what, not the hype: "Sprint 11: Visual Content Engine" not "Revolutionizing Content Creation"
- Prefer specific numbers over vague claims: "64 tools across 12 modules" not "Powerful tool ecosystem"
- Use the em-dash (—) for rhythm: "Sprint 11 — Visual Content Engine"

**Subtitle rules:**
- Expand the headline with one concrete detail
- Technical vocabulary is welcome: "Puppeteer rendering, FFmpeg assembly, hybrid MCP routing"
- No marketing fluff: never "game-changing", "cutting-edge", "revolutionary"

**Footer/metadata style:**
- JetBrains Mono
- Format: `atharvasingh.dev` or `ai-os v2.0` or `2026.03.19`
- Dates in ISO-ish format: `2026.03.19` not "March 19, 2026"

**Word bank (use these):** shipped, deployed, built, live, operational, integrated, orchestrated, automated, surfaced
**Avoid:** launched, excited, proud, game-changing, revolutionary, next-gen, powered by AI (ironic given the project, but this phrase is dead)

## Context B — Bharatvarsh: Cinematic Weight

**Voice:** A world speaking through its artifacts. Ominous, atmospheric, sparse.

**Headline rules:**
- Bebas Neue ALL CAPS. Always.
- Maximum 5 words. Ideally 2-3. Let the visual do the storytelling.
- Declarative or imperative: "THE NEXUS AWAITS" not "Discover the Nexus"
- Fragment sentences are powerful: "A TRUTH BURIED." "THE RECKONING."

**Subtitle rules:**
- Lower contrast (muted text color), like a whispered aside
- One sentence maximum. Evocative, not explanatory.
- "The truth behind the Rathore dynasty" not "In this chapter, we explore..."

**Badge/label rules:**
- Short and classificatory: "NEW CHAPTER", "DECLASSIFIED", "REDACTED", "FACTION: BHARATSENA"
- Use the intelligence document aesthetic: clearance levels, file stamps, classification markers

**Footer/metadata style:**
- JetBrains Mono
- Format: `@welcometobharatvarsh` or `BHARATVARSH` or `2026 // CHAPTER 5`
- Can use // as separator for the surveillance-document feel

**Word bank:** awaits, buried, rewritten, emerges, falls, rises, truth, shadow, faction, nexus, legacy, reckoning, protocol, classified, declassified
**Avoid:** amazing, incredible, can't wait, dropping soon, link in bio, check it out, stay tuned (social media marketing speak kills the atmosphere)

## Context C — Portfolio: Conversational Authority

**Voice:** A senior tech leader sharing insight. Confident but approachable, never arrogant.

**Headline rules:**
- Sentence case, Inter font
- Personal and specific: "Building AI Agents That Build Themselves" not "The Future of AI Agents"
- First-person framing works: "How I Architected..." or "What I Learned..."
- Contrarian or insight-led: challenge assumptions, share a non-obvious take

**Subtitle rules:**
- Expand with credibility: mention a specific project, metric, or tool
- "How I designed a personal operating system with 64 tools and zero vendor lock-in"

**Footer/metadata style:**
- JetBrains Mono
- Format: `Atharva Singh` or `AI & Cloud` or the specific platform handle

**Word bank:** architected, designed, built, shipped, scaled, discovered, learned, evolved, operational, zero-to-one
**Avoid:** guru, ninja, rockstar, 10x, hustle, grind, thought leader (self-applying this term kills it)

## Cross-Context Copy Rules

1. **No exclamation marks in headlines.** Ever. Exclamation marks signal desperation.
2. **No emoji in visual assets.** Text-only. Emoji belongs in social captions, not in the rendered image.
3. **CTA text is action-specific:** "Read Chapter 5" not "Learn More". "View the Dashboard" not "Click Here".
4. **Every piece of text must earn its pixel space.** If removing a word doesn't change the meaning, remove it.
5. **Numbers are always specific:** "891 contacts" not "hundreds of contacts". "64 tools" not "dozens of tools".
