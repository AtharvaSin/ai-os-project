# AI&U -- First 10 Video Library Plan

## Purpose of the First Library

The first 10 videos are not just content -- they are a channel definition system. They tell the audience what AI&U is about, what quality level to expect, what topics will recur, and why the creator is worth following. A new visitor should be able to understand the channel within 3-5 viewed videos. The recommended balance is 6 evergreen foundation videos, 3 practical build videos, and 1 flagship manifesto.

## Video 1: AI&U Manifesto (Flagship)

- **Pillar:** All three
- **Level:** Flagship positioning
- **Topic:** What AI&U is, what the channel believes, and why practical AI matters more than AI hype
- **Purpose:** Define the channel identity. Set expectations. Establish the creator's authority and voice. This is the anchor reference for the entire channel.

## Video 2: Where to Use AI vs. Manual Effort (Decision Rule)

- **Pillar:** P1 -- AI for Common Person
- **Level:** Searchable long-form
- **Topic:** A framework for deciding when AI helps, when it hurts, and when manual effort is still better
- **Purpose:** Foundational judgment content. Sets the channel's credibility as a place for decisions, not just demos.

## Video 3: How to Use AI for Structured Research (Tool Leverage)

- **Pillar:** P2 -- Using AI Tools
- **Level:** Searchable long-form
- **Topic:** Step-by-step walkthrough of using AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) for research that produces reliable, structured output
- **Purpose:** Evergreen. High search potential. Demonstrates the channel's outcome-first teaching style.

## Video 4: Build Your First AI Automation with n8n (Workflow Build)

- **Pillar:** P3 -- Building AI Workflows
- **Level:** Deeper workflow
- **Topic:** Practical build video showing a no-code AI automation from scratch using n8n
- **Purpose:** Proves the channel teaches real implementation, not theory. Strong thumbnail potential.

## Video 5: AI Skills That Actually Matter in 2026 (Future Readiness)

- **Pillar:** P1 -- AI for Common Person
- **Level:** Searchable long-form
- **Topic:** Which AI skills are worth learning for students, professionals, and enthusiasts -- and which are overrated
- **Purpose:** High search volume topic. Addresses all three audience segments directly.

## Video 6: Using AI in Coding Workflows (Tool Leverage)

- **Pillar:** P2 -- Using AI Tools
- **Level:** Searchable long-form
- **Topic:** How to use AI coding assistants (Copilot, Claude Code, Cursor) effectively -- what to trust, what to verify, workflow patterns
- **Purpose:** Appeals to technical audience. Demonstrates code-aware credibility.

## Video 7: Build a RAG Chatbot from Scratch (Workflow Build)

- **Pillar:** P3 -- Building AI Workflows
- **Level:** Deeper workflow
- **Topic:** Architecture walkthrough and build of a Retrieval-Augmented Generation chatbot, from no-code prototype to code upgrade
- **Purpose:** Flagship Pillar 3 video. Diagram-first, systems explanation, implementation-safe guidance.

## Video 8: AI for Presentations and Visual Work (Tool Leverage)

- **Pillar:** P2 -- Using AI Tools
- **Level:** Searchable long-form
- **Topic:** Using AI tools for storyboarding, presentation building, and visual generation
- **Purpose:** Broad appeal. Visual demonstration of tangible output improvement.

## Video 9: When AI Output Is Unsafe to Trust (Decision Rule)

- **Pillar:** P1 -- AI for Common Person
- **Level:** Searchable long-form
- **Topic:** Where AI confidently produces wrong answers -- hallucination patterns, verification methods, and trust frameworks
- **Purpose:** Credibility builder. Shows the channel is honest about AI limitations, not just a promoter.

## Video 10: Build an AI Agent with Approval Loops (Workflow Build)

- **Pillar:** P3 -- Building AI Workflows
- **Level:** Deeper workflow
- **Topic:** Designing an AI agent with human-in-the-loop approval, error handling, and safe autonomy boundaries
- **Purpose:** Advanced build content. Demonstrates the expert-in-the-loop philosophy that differentiates the channel.

## Shorts Extraction

Every long-form video should be mined for 3-5 Shorts. Best short-form categories: myth busts, workflow reveals, rules of thumb, one-step automations, dangerous mistakes, and underrated use cases. Each Short should feel complete on its own but point naturally to the related long-form video.
