# AI&U -- Content Production Workflow

## Overview

AI&U production follows a six-phase pipeline designed to make video creation repeatable and systematic. The channel should operate like a content operating system rather than a loose list of ideas. Each phase has clear inputs, outputs, and quality gates.

## Phase 0: Topic Qualification

Before any production work begins, every topic must pass through a qualification filter:
- **Viewer relevance** -- Does it solve a real problem or answer a recurring question?
- **Strategic fit** -- Does it belong to one of the three pillars?
- **Content leverage** -- Can it produce one strong long-form video, at least three Shorts, and reusable graphics?
- **Differentiation** -- Can AI&U cover it with a stronger practical angle than typical channels?
- **Execution feasibility** -- Can it be produced with available tools, examples, and assets?

If a topic fails multiple filters, it does not enter the active build queue.

## Phase 1: Structure and Script

Build the video architecture before recording:
- Write the hook (first 10-20 seconds combining promise, tension, and practical payoff)
- Create the chapter map with mini-promise labels (not generic "intro/main/conclusion")
- Draft the narration sequence following the standard structure: hook, viewer value, problem framing, core framework, examples/demos, failure modes, practical summary, CTA
- Plan on-screen visuals, diagrams, and demos for each section

## Phase 2: Asset Preparation

Prepare all supporting materials:
- Record or prepare demos and screen recordings
- Create prompt packs, templates, or repos if relevant
- Generate infographics, workflow diagrams, and charts
- Prepare data or examples for live demonstrations

## Phase 3: Recording

Current production setup:
- Camera: phone with external microphone
- Software: Prism Live Studio for multi-source capture
- Sources: facecam + screen recording + slides/visuals
- Focus on natural delivery with the consultant-builder-teacher voice

## Phase 4: Editing

Editing environment: DaVinci Resolve. Key editing priorities:
- Fast, compelling first 30 seconds
- Strong pattern interrupts to maintain engagement
- Clear chapter transitions
- Layered visuals (not just talking head)
- Pacing discipline -- cut anything that slows momentum

## Phase 5: Publishing

Complete publish package for every video:
- Title (searchable or intriguing pattern)
- Thumbnail (following one of three templates: Big Promise + Proof, Before vs After, or Workflow Diagram)
- Description with keywords and links
- Timestamps/chapters with mini-promise labels
- Pinned comment with additional resources
- End-screen routing to the next relevant video
- Playlist placement for series continuity

## Phase 6: Review and Improve

Post-publish analysis (twice weekly cadence):
- Click-through rate (packaging quality)
- Audience retention curve (content quality and pacing)
- Retention dips and spikes (identify weak and strong segments)
- Traffic source mix (discovery health)
- Subscriber conversion (content-to-follow quality)
- Returning viewers (loyalty indicator)

Use findings to iterate on future videos. Diagnose patterns: high CTR + low retention means packaging mismatch; low CTR + solid retention means the content is good but packaging is weak.
