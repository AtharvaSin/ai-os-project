# Phase 1 Marketing Strategy -- Lore Reveals

## Strategic Context

Bharatvarsh marketing does not follow traditional book marketing patterns. The novel markets like a world, not a product. Phase 1 focuses on building audience awareness and intrigue through lore reveals -- surfacing details of the Bharatvarsh universe to create curiosity before pushing purchase CTAs. The principle is simple: pull people into the world first, and the desire to read the book follows organically.

## Brand Voice for Lore Reveals

Lore reveals use the "In-Universe Mode" of the Bharatvarsh brand voice:
- Intelligence dossier tone -- classified, precise, controlled
- Atmospheric and immersive -- pull people into the world
- Questions, not answers -- provoke curiosity
- Vocabulary: engineered calm, The Mesh, Directorate, doctrine, compliance, blind spots, surveillance, freedom, truth, sacrifice
- Never spoil plot points. Tease, do not tell.
- Respect the Classified/Declassified status system from the Bible. Do not reveal classified information publicly.

## Content Pillars for Phase 1

Phase 1 lore marketing draws from five content pillars:

1. **World Windows** (2x/week) -- Peeks into the Bharatvarsh world. Mechanics, society, daily life, technology. Example: "The Mesh sees everything. Every transaction, every movement, every word spoken in the open."
2. **Character Spotlights** (1x/week) -- Focus on one character with mystery and conflict. Example: Kahaan as "the military prince" with a visual of tactical lens overlay.
3. **The Question** (1x/week) -- Provocative prompts tied to themes. Example: "What would you sacrifice for the truth?" These work on Twitter, Instagram Stories, and the forum.
4. **Behind the Build** (1x/week) -- Author perspective on writing, world-building, and the technology behind the website. This is Author Mode voice, not in-universe.
5. **Gateway CTAs** (1-2x/week) -- Direct invitations to buy, visit the website, try Bhoomi AI, or join the forum. CTAs should feel like invitations, not advertisements.

## Platform Execution

### Twitter/X -- Primary Reach Engine
- Content mix: 40% lore reveals, 20% world contrasts, 15% character teasers, 15% author insights, 10% CTAs
- Format: 3-4 tweets/week minimum, mixing single tweets and threads
- Hashtags: #Bharatvarsh #MahaBharatvarsh #AlternateHistory #IndianSciFi (max 3 per tweet)
- Engagement: Reply to every comment in the first hour. Quote-tweet reader theories.

### Instagram -- Visual Storytelling
- Content mix: 40% character/world art, 25% quote cards, 20% behind-the-scenes, 15% interactive
- Visual style: Dark, atmospheric, obsidian/powder/mustard palette matching the website. Cinematic feel.
- Format: 2-3 feed posts/week. Stories for polls, Q&A, behind-the-scenes.

### LinkedIn -- Author-as-Builder
- Content mix: 60% author-builder journey, 25% tech behind the novel (Bhoomi AI, website), 15% AI-and-storytelling commentary
- Voice: Professional, reflective, systems-thinking angle
- Format: 1-2 posts/week, 150-300 words

## First Five Content Ideas

1. **Twitter Lore Reveal:** "The Mesh -- what it sees, how it works." Atmospheric, mysterious. First post candidate.
2. **Instagram Character Teaser:** "Kahaan -- the military prince." Visual: dark portrait, tactical lens overlay.
3. **LinkedIn Behind-the-Scenes:** "Building Bhoomi AI -- unprecedented for a novel site." Tech angle: LiveKit + Vertex AI.
4. **Twitter World Contrast:** "Children think checkpoints are streetlights." Provocative one-liner.
5. **Forum Community Prompt:** "What would you sacrifice for truth?" Seed the forum with the novel's core question.

## Rules and Guardrails

- One dedicated CTA post per 4-5 content posts. Do not over-push purchases.
- Even CTAs should feel atmospheric, not salesy. Frame the lead magnet as "access a classified dossier," not "download a free preview."
- Do not use AI-generated images that look obviously AI-generated. The brand is atmospheric and cinematic, not plastic.
- Every post should work standalone. A first-time viewer should be intrigued without needing prior context.
- Do not copy other book marketing patterns. Bharatvarsh markets like a world.
