# AI&U — Content System and Video Architecture

## 1. Content system objective
The content system exists to make the channel repeatable.

Without a system, each video becomes a fresh creative struggle.
With a system, each video becomes a modular build.

AI&U should therefore be run like a **content operating system**, not a loose list of ideas.

The system should help answer:

- what topics to cover
- how to package them
- how to structure them
- how to extract Shorts
- how to sequence videos into a library

---

## 2. The channel's content ladder
The channel should operate across four levels of content depth.

## Level 1 — Hooks / Shorts
Purpose:
- attract new viewers
- isolate one insight
- create curiosity toward long-form

Format:
- one rule
- one example
- one punchline or takeaway

---

## Level 2 — Searchable long-form basics
Purpose:
- capture steady discovery
- build foundational authority

Examples:
- how to use AI for X
- where to use AI and where not
- AI workflow for X role

---

## Level 3 — Deeper workflow videos
Purpose:
- demonstrate implementation maturity
- build trust with serious viewers

Examples:
- build an AI workflow in n8n
- prototype an AI assistant
- use AI tools across a project lifecycle

---

## Level 4 — Flagship positioning videos
Purpose:
- define the channel's worldview
- become anchor references
- deepen brand differentiation

Examples:
- AI&U manifesto
- practical AI skills to stay relevant
- the real operating model for using AI

---

## 3. Topic qualification framework
Every topic should pass through a qualification filter before being produced.

### Filter 1 — Viewer relevance
Does the topic solve a real problem or answer a recurring question?

### Filter 2 — Strategic fit
Does it belong to one of the three pillars?

### Filter 3 — Content leverage
Can it produce:
- one strong long-form video
- at least three Shorts
- reusable graphics or frameworks

### Filter 4 — Differentiation
Can AI&U cover it with a stronger practical angle than typical channels?

### Filter 5 — Execution feasibility
Can it be realistically produced with available tools, examples, and assets?

If a topic fails multiple filters, it should not enter the active build queue.

---

## 4. Standard long-form structure
Long-form videos should be standardized around a strong explanation sequence.

## Recommended core structure
1. Hook
2. What this video will do for the viewer
3. Why the problem matters
4. Core framework / explanation
5. Real examples or demos
6. Common mistakes / failure modes
7. Practical summary / checklist
8. CTA to the next relevant video or asset

### Hook design
The first 10–20 seconds should combine:
- promise
- tension
- practical payoff

### Middle-section design
The middle should avoid “flat explanation.”
It should cycle through:
- point
- example
- visual
- implication

### Ending design
The ending should do two jobs:
- compress the learning
- route the viewer to the next logical piece of content

## 6. The 10-video foundation logic
The early channel should be built like a mini curriculum.

### Recommended balance
- 6 evergreen foundation videos
- 3 practical build videos
- 1 flagship manifesto / positioning video

### Purpose of the first library
The first library is not just content.
It is a **channel definition system**.

It tells the audience:
- what the channel is about
- what quality level to expect
- what topics will recur
- why the creator is worth following

### Functional requirement
The initial library should allow a new visitor to understand the channel within 3–5 viewed videos.

## 8. Packaging system
Packaging is part of the content system, not an afterthought.

### Titles should generally fall into two modes
#### Searchable
Examples of the pattern:
- How to ___ with AI
- AI workflow for ___
- Best AI method for ___

#### Intriguing
Examples of the pattern:
- Stop using AI for ___
- Most people use AI wrong for ___
- The smarter way to use AI for ___

### Thumbnail rule
One emotion + one object + 2–4 words.

### Packaging quality rule
The title and thumbnail should create curiosity without breaking promise integrity.

If CTR is good but retention collapses early, the packaging is likely overpromising or misframing the video.

---

## 9. Chapter architecture
Chapters are mandatory for the channel because they support:

- better skimmability
- perceived structure
- easier revisits
- stronger educational feel

### Channel-specific chapter philosophy
Chapters should not be generic.
They should feel like mini-promises.

Instead of weak chapter labels like:
- intro
- main part
- conclusion

use stronger labels like:
- Where AI actually helps
- The 3 filters that matter
- The workflow most people skip

---

## 10. Shorts extraction logic
Every long-form video should be mined for Shorts.

### What makes a good Short clip
- one sharp claim
- one concrete example
- one surprising rule
- one mistake/fix moment
- one “before vs after” insight

### Best short-form categories for AI&U
- myth busts
- workflow reveals
- rules of thumb
- one-step automations
- dangerous mistakes
- underrated use cases

### Shorts quality bar
A Short should feel complete on its own, but also point naturally to a related long-form video.

---

## 11. Series design
The channel should increasingly move from one-off videos to series.

### Why series matter
They:
- improve viewer routing
- deepen authority
- simplify future ideation
- create repeatable branding

### Good recurring series examples
- AI for [specific role]
- Build this AI workflow
- AI myths that waste your time
- future-ready AI skills
- no-code first, code next

---

## 12. Scripting rules
Scripts should follow a few permanent rules.

### Rule 1
Always state what the viewer gets.

### Rule 2
Use frameworks and labels that are easy to remember.

### Rule 3
Alternate explanation with proof:
- example
- demo
- scenario
- diagram

### Rule 4
Name the failure modes.

### Rule 5
Keep the viewer moving toward a useful next step.

---

## 13. CTA strategy
CTAs should change with the type of video.

### For concept videos
Best CTA:
- watch the next related explainer
- follow the series

### For tool videos
Best CTA:
- get the prompt pack / template / repo
- watch the advanced follow-up

### For workflow videos
Best CTA:
- get the blueprint
- see the no-code or code variant
- reach out for implementation discussion

The CTA should feel like a natural next step, not an interruption.

---

## 14. What “good content” means for AI&U
A strong AI&U video should do five things at once:

1. be clear
2. be useful
3. feel structured
4. prove credibility
5. create the next content opportunity

That is the standard the system should protect.
