# Bharatvarsh Content — Lore Accuracy Rules

> **Purpose:** Hard constraints on timeline and lore accuracy for all Bharatvarsh content creation. Prevents anachronisms and timeline misallocations across posts, visuals, and marketing materials. Consult this file alongside BHARATVARSH_TIMELINE.md before assigning dates to any in-universe event referenced in content.
>
> **Created:** 2026-03-22
> **Authority:** Canon — violations break continuity.

---

## Rule 1: Temple/Worship-Site Repurposing Follows the Religion Ban

**Canon sequence:**
1. **1978** — The One-Year Emergency. Army assumes permanent control via civilian handover.
2. **1978–1980** — The Great Quieting. Military patrols, curfews, riot-leader arrests.
3. **1984** — Tribhuj Puran banned. Religion, caste surnames, and independent media outlawed simultaneously. Houses of worship **begin** repurposing in the same year: mosques → cancer centres, temples → senate chambers, churches → government buildings.

**Rule:** Any content referencing a specific temple/mosque/church repurposing must use a date **in or after 1984**. The ban and repurposing begin simultaneously per canon. Major, nationally significant sites were among the first to be converted (1984 itself), with the rollout continuing over subsequent years for smaller sites.

**Suggested date ranges for repurposing orders:**
- Major sites (national significance): **1984** (same year as the decree — first wave)
- Regional sites: 1985–1988
- Local/small sites: 1988–1995
- By the mid-1990s, essentially all public worship spaces have been converted

**Example:** The Lakshmanpur Mandir (Senate Chamber 7) is a major nationally significant site → repurposing directive correctly references **1984** as a first-wave conversion.

---

## Rule 2: Army Takeover Precedes All Military-Era Policies

No military directive, Directorate order, or "National Cohesion Directive" can be dated before **1978**. The civilian government was still in power (however dysfunctionally) before the Emergency handover. Content referencing Directorate authority, military tribunals, Mesh deployment, or curfew enforcement must use dates from 1978 onward.

---

## Rule 3: Mesh Deployment Phases Are Sequential

| Phase | Period | Capability |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Seeds | c. 1980 | First cameras, biometric IDs |
| Phase 1 | 2000–2005 | Bracecomms (military), camera expansion, biometric e-wallet pilot |
| Phase 2 | 2005–2010 | Hovercams, algorithmic checkpoints, smart-street infrastructure |
| Phase 3 | 2010–2015 | Full coverage, civilian Bracecomms, sentiment mining, micro-expression recognition |

Content must not show hovercams or algorithmic checkpoints in scenes set before 2005, or full civilian Bracecomms before 2010.

---

## Rule 4: Tribhuj Existence Phases

| Phase | Period | Status |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Ancient monastic order | Pre-1978 | Legitimate ascetic martial wing |
| Modern Tribhuj (Rudra's) | c. 1990s | Pro-democracy resistance front |
| Defeated / disbanded | c. 2000–2003 | Rudra exiles to Nepal |
| Dormant | 2003–2025 | No active organisation |
| Impostor Tribhuj | Oct 2025 | Fake brand used by state-manufactured bombers |

Content must never imply Tribhuj was active between 2003 and October 2025. The Grey Trident graffiti can appear as residual cultural memory or copycat acts, but never as organised movement activity during the dormant period.

---

## Rule 5: KACHA Programme Is Post-2010

The KACHA programme (neural-diode implant research, Facility IV, Venus glyph) begins c. 2010–2015. No content should reference KACHA technology, A-Delta/B-Omega collars, or Venus glyphs in scenes set before 2010. Additionally, KACHA is **[CLASSIFIED]** — public-facing content must never reveal its existence, scope, or connection to the 20-10 bombings.

---

## Rule 6: Spoiler Safety (Restated for Completeness)

Never reveal in any public content:
- The KACHA programme's existence or nature
- Pratap's orchestration of the 20-10 bombings
- Kahaan's final choice to surrender command
- The power transfer to Rudra
- Facility IV or Dr. Sahil Arshad's role
