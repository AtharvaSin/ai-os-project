## 1. Brand Voice

### Tone
The Bharatvarsh marketing voice operates on a spectrum between two modes:

**In-Universe Mode** (for lore reveals, character teasers, world-building posts):
- Intelligence dossier tone — classified, precise, controlled
- Atmospheric and immersive — pull people into the world
- Questions, not answers — provoke curiosity
- "What would you sacrifice for the truth?"

**Author Mode** (for behind-the-scenes, craft, tech, personal):
- Builder-storyteller — someone who constructed an entire world
- Technically impressive but accessible — "I built an AI voice assistant for a novel website"
- Passionate but not desperate — confidence without hype
- Invites the reader in rather than pushing the product

### Vocabulary
Use: engineered calm, The Mesh, Directorate, doctrine, compliance, blind spots, surveillance, freedom, truth, sacrifice
Avoid: "thrilling page-turner," "must-read," "epic," "gripping" — generic book marketing language

### Rules
- Never spoil plot points. Tease, don't tell.
- Respect Classified/Declassified status from the Bible — don't reveal classified information publicly.
- Every post should work standalone — a first-time viewer should be intrigued without needing prior context.
- Purchase CTAs should feel like invitations, not advertisements.

## 2. Target Audiences

### Audience A: Indian Sci-Fi / Speculative Fiction Readers
**Who:** 18-35, English-reading, consumed Amish Tripathi / Devdutt Pattanaik / Netflix Indian sci-fi
**Where:** Instagram, Twitter/X, Amazon browse, Goodreads
**What hooks them:** "Indian alternate history" + "what if India never was colonized" + surveillance state themes
**Best content types:** Lore reveals, world-contrast posts, quote cards

### Audience B: Global Alternate History / Dystopian Fans
**Who:** 20-40, fans of 1984, Man in the High Castle, Brave New World, Black Mirror
**Where:** Reddit (r/alternatehistory, r/worldbuilding), Twitter/X, Goodreads
**What hooks them:** Surveillance state mechanics, political philosophy, world-building depth
**Best content types:** World-contrast posts, timeline deep-dives, Bhoomi AI demo

### Audience C: Tech-Curious / AI-Interested Readers
**Who:** 22-40, follow AI/tech content, intrigued by the intersection of fiction and technology
**Where:** Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Product Hunt, Hacker News
**What hooks them:** "A novel with an AI voice assistant" + "immersive transmedia website" + author-as-builder angle
**Best content types:** Behind-the-scenes tech posts, Bhoomi AI demos, website feature showcases

### Audience D: Indian Book Community
**Who:** Active readers, book bloggers, bookstagrammers, book-club members
**Where:** Instagram (#bookstagram), Twitter/X (#IndianAuthors), WhatsApp book groups
**What hooks them:** Author story, Indian publishing indie journey, beautiful book aesthetics
**Best content types:** Author posts, review requests, community engagement, Q&A

## 3. Platform Strategies

### Twitter/X — Reach & Engagement Engine
**Goal:** Build audience through short-form lore content and world-building threads
**Content mix:** 40% lore reveals, 20% world contrasts, 15% character teasers, 15% author insights, 10% CTAs
**Posting:** 3-4 tweets/week minimum. Mix single tweets and threads.
**Engagement:** Reply to every comment in first hour. Quote-tweet interesting reader theories.
**Hashtags:** #Bharatvarsh #MahaBharatvarsh #AlternateHistory #IndianSciFi #IndianAuthor — max 3 per tweet

### Instagram — Visual Storytelling
**Goal:** Build aesthetic brand presence and drive book discovery
**Content mix:** 40% character/world art, 25% quote cards, 20% behind-the-scenes, 15% community/interactive
**Visual style:** Dark, atmospheric, obsidian/powder/mustard palette matching the website. High contrast. Cinematic feel.
**Posting:** 2-3 feed posts/week. Stories for polls, Q&A, behind-the-scenes.
**Hashtags:** 15-20 per post in first comment. Mix broad (#scifi #alternatehistory) with niche (#indianscifi #bharatvarsh)

### LinkedIn — Author-as-Builder Platform
**Goal:** Professional credibility + reach the tech-curious audience
**Content mix:** 60% author-builder journey, 25% tech behind the novel, 15% industry commentary (AI + storytelling)
**Voice:** Professional, reflective, systems-thinking angle. Not salesy.
**Posting:** 1-2 posts/week. Longer form (150-300 words).

### Bharatvarsh Forum — Community Depth
**Goal:** Convert readers into engaged community members
**Strategy:** Seed with 10+ discussion topics before promoting. Cover: theories, character analysis, alternate history debates, world-building questions.
**Moderation:** Vertex AI + manual review.

## 7. What Not to Do

- Don't over-post purchase CTAs. One dedicated CTA post per 4-5 content posts.
- Don't break the in-universe tone with "BUY NOW" language. Even CTAs should feel atmospheric.
- Don't reveal classified lore elements in public marketing content.
- Don't use AI-generated images that look obviously AI-generated. The brand is atmospheric and cinematic, not plastic.
- Don't copy other book marketing patterns. Bharatvarsh's strength is that it doesn't market like a typical book — it markets like a world.
