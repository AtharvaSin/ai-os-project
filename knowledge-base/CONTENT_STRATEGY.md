# Bharatvarsh Content Strategy — Campaign Content Pipeline

> **Canonical strategy document for the Bharatvarsh Content Operations Platform (BCOP).**
> This document governs how every piece of promotional content is conceived, filtered, shaped, and delivered. All future post generation — whether manual or automated — must follow this pipeline. Any new content calendar entry must trace its lineage through the layers defined here.

---

## 1. Purpose & Philosophy

This campaign acts like **dropping snippets of information** that give followers deeper knowledge about the Bharatvarsh alternate-timeline universe, its general history, and its story world. The aim is to create **sensation and intrigue** — not direct book promotion.

Every post should feel like a fragment intercepted from the world itself: a leaked document, a photograph smuggled out, graffiti on a wall, a news clip from Bharatvarsh's own media. The audience should feel like they are **discovering** a world, not being sold a book.

**Core Principles:**
- Spoiler-safe: No post reveals major plot twists or the novel's ending
- World-first: Build the universe; let the novel sell itself through curiosity
- In-world voice: Content comes *from* Bharatvarsh, not *about* Bharatvarsh
- Long-term sequencing: Each post is a move in a larger narrative chess game
- Canon-locked: Every fact must trace to the Bharatvarsh Bible, Timeline, Characters, or Locations docs

## 3. Layer 2 — Story Angles

Three spoiler-safe extraction lenses that determine which part of the story world a post focuses on. Each angle has a distinct voice, visual palette, and emotional register.

### 3.1 Bharatsena Angle (The State)

**Focus:** The surveillance Mesh, advanced technology augmenting daily life and military operations, army control and discipline, internal operations, the engineered utopia.

**Voice:** Official, clinical, subtly menacing. The Mesh or state apparatus speaking directly. Confident in its own benevolence.

**Visual palette:** Navy (#0D1B2A), Powder-blue (#BFD7EA), Hologram Cyan (#17D0E3), Mustard accent (#FFB703). Clean surfaces, institutional precision, surveillance HUD elements.

**Lore sources:**
- Bible: The Mesh (Section 5), Political Structure (Section 3), Economy & Technology (Section 4)
- Timeline: Era 5 (Engineered Peace), Mesh deployment phases
- Locations: Indrapur HQ, Lakshmanpur, checkpoint typologies
- Characters: Kahaan (as state instrument), Pratap, Tara

**Example topics:** Mesh daily operations reports, Bracecomm telemetry, OxyPole status updates, checkpoint compliance data, curfew protocols, Bharatsena recruitment, military technology showcases, citizen convenience metrics.

### 3.2 Akakpen Angle (The Wild)

**Focus:** Life in the Eastern & Himalayan Treaty Zone. Smaller tribes living closer to nature but with advanced spy tech and guerrilla warfare tactics. Outside Bharatvarsh's direct control. The alternative.

**Voice:** Quieter, earthier, poetic. Nature imagery juxtaposed with tactical precision. A world that chose freedom over convenience.

**Visual palette:** Deep Greens (#162B18, #223F1C), Bark Brown (#4E3B2A), Functional Orange (#D9781E micro-accent), Marigold (#FFC933). Mountain terrain, forests, handcraft textures, natural light, fog and cedar.

**Lore sources:**
- Bible: Administrative Blocs — Eastern & Himalayan Treaty Zone (Section 3.2), Teesta Treaty
- Timeline: Era 4 (Eastern guerrilla stalemate), Teesta Treaty
- Characters: Rudra (exile years), Kaali, Arshi, the tribal community
- Locations: Treaty Zone, Nepal-Treaty Hills, mountain settlements

**Example topics:** Treaty Zone geography and customs, tribal self-governance, no-fly valleys, guerrilla training traditions, Rudra's exile life, Akakpen culture, the contrast between natural freedom and state convenience, the drone-jammer sanctuaries.

### 3.3 Tribhuj Angle (The Resistance)

**Focus:** Old Tribhuj — the banned democratic resistance movement deemed a terrorist organisation by Bharatsena. New Tribhuj — the hijacked name resurging as the 20-10 bombings. The tension between the original ideal and its violent co-option.

**Voice:** Urgent, idealistic, conflicted. The tension between hope and desperation. Sometimes a whisper on a wall; sometimes a broadcast interruption.

**Visual palette:** Grey trident on orange cloth, neon-geometric corrupted trident (New Tribhuj), deep shadows, underground textures, spray-paint aesthetics, stolen broadcast static.

**Lore sources:**
- Bible: Tribhuj Puran ban (1984), Section 3.4 (Law & Order)
- Timeline: Era 3-4 (Tribhuj founding, defeat, exile), Era 6 (20-10 bombings, BVN hijack)
- Characters: Rudra (as founder), Jwala, Adil (studio hijack), the pamphlet rain
- Events: Festival Fortnight pamphlet drop, BVN-24x7 broadcast hijack

**Example topics:** The original Tribhuj creed, the trident symbol's meaning, the 1984 ban, graffiti sightings, intercepted pamphlets, the question of who really bombed the seven cities, the difference between old Tribhuj and new Tribhuj, underground faith practices.

## 4. Layer 3 — Distillation Filters

Once an idea is extracted through a story angle, it passes through one of three thematic filters that determine the *type* of conversation the post creates. Filters can be combined with any angle.

### 4.1 Living Without Religion

**Purpose:** Introduce the audience to the fact that religion is outlawed in Bharatvarsh. Explore what society looks like when faith is state-managed — mosques become hospitals, temples become senate halls, open evangelism equals sedition.

**Emotional register:** Thought-provoking, unsettling, philosophical. Neither condemning nor endorsing — presenting the reality and letting the audience decide.

**Key lore anchors:**
- 1984: Houses of worship repurposed (mosque → cancer centre, Kalighat temple → senate)
- Caste surnames banned
- Open evangelism = sedition
- Devout citizens conduct rituals in basements
- Heritage Micro-Sets in each city (one repurposed building per faith)
- Contraband religious art in underground economies

**Angle combinations:**
- Bharatsena + Religion → Official reports framing it as progressive ("We are beyond superstition")
- Akakpen + Religion → The Treaty Zone as the last place where faith survives openly
- Tribhuj + Religion → Underground worship, smuggled sacred texts, the Tribhuj Puran ban

### 4.2 Bharatvarsh Progress in Medical & Military

**Purpose:** Inform the audience that because the 1717 divergence prevented colonisation, Bharatvarsh is a global superpower — world leader in medical and military technology. Emphasise the scale of advancement: anti-ageing biotech, cybernetics, green-tech exports, UNSC veto power.

**Emotional register:** Awe mixed with unease. The technology is genuinely impressive, but the defence-first cycle means civilians live a generation behind soldiers. The progress comes at a cost.

**Key lore anchors:**
- 1717 divergence and its cascading consequences
- Manhattan Project co-partnership
- UNSC permanent seat with veto
- Anti-ageing serum (Pratap at 85 looking 65)
- Neural-Diode Lattice, KACHA programme
- Defence-first cycle (5-15 year military classification before civilian release)
- Green-tech exports praised internationally
- Bio-medical complex: memory-editing chips, advanced prostheses
- Cybernetics pipeline: military-first, civilian-second

**Angle combinations:**
- Bharatsena + Progress → Technology showcases, medical breakthroughs, global standing reports
- Akakpen + Progress → What the tribes developed independently — spy tech, terrain intelligence, drone-jammers
- Tribhuj + Progress → The dark side of progress — KACHA, weaponised cybernetics, the cost of enhancement

### 4.3 Novel Intro Items

**Purpose:** Bring the audience closer to actual events and premises from the novel's storyline. Create awareness of the narrative without spoiling it. Tease characters, conflicts, and mysteries.

**Emotional register:** Suspenseful, cinematic, narrative-driven. Each post is a scene fragment or dossier entry that makes the audience want to know what happens next.

**Key lore anchors:**
- The 20-10 bombings (seven cities, coordinated)
- Kahaan's assignment to lead the investigation
- Character introductions (Kahaan, Rudra, Pratap, Kaali, Arshi, Hana)
- The central mystery: who attacks a nation that watches everything?
- The BVN-24x7 studio hijack
- The Festive Fortnight pamphlet drop
- Facility IV and KACHA (teased, never fully revealed)

**Angle combinations:**
- Bharatsena + Novel → Kahaan's investigation, military response, official reports on the bombings
- Akakpen + Novel → Rudra's return from exile, Kaali's injury, the tribal response
- Tribhuj + Novel → The pamphlet rain, the broadcast hijack, the question of who co-opted the name

## 5. Layer 4 — Content Channels

The final layer fits the filtered idea into one of three fixed output formats. Each channel has a rigid design framework that ensures visual consistency across the campaign.

### 5.1 Channel 1: Declassified Reports (Formal Documents)

**Format:** Posts designed as snippets of breaking news events, excerpts from official documents, evidence items like CCTV images, leaked memos, intelligence briefs, compliance reports.

**Design types:** Carousels (multi-slide deep dives) or single photos with key image + textual/react overlays around edges for headings and data enhancement.

**Caption style:** Elaborate. Deep-dive text providing context, analysis, and world-building detail. The caption does heavy narrative lifting.

**Visual framework:**
- Key image (AI-generated, Jim Lee style) as centrepiece
- React-rendered background patterns and overlays (surveillance grids, HUD elements, document borders)
- Classification stamps (DECLASSIFIED, REDACTED, CLASSIFIED headers)
- Institutional typography, data readouts, reference numbers
- Brand tokens as CSS variables in templates

**Best for:** Bharatsena angle posts, technology deep-dives, investigation updates, compliance reports, medical/military progress showcases.

### 5.2 Channel 2: Wall Graffiti & Photography Posters

**Format:** The most artistic channel. Single (or multi) images — stills, graffiti, portraits, scenery, close-ups. Images that invoke strong emotions and follow composition design principles.

**Design types:** Single image posts or small galleries. Composition-driven, emotionally evocative.

**Caption style:** Short to medium. High-quality, impactful quotes or brief poetic text. Let the image carry the weight.

**Visual framework:**
- AI-generated imagery following Jim Lee art style to the core
- Graffiti compositions (spray-painted tridents on walls, protest art, banned symbols)
- Photographic compositions (portraits, landscapes, atmospheric close-ups)
- Strong composition principles: rule of thirds, leading lines, contrast, negative space
- Emotional impact prioritised over information density

**Best for:** Akakpen angle posts, character reveals, atmospheric world-building, Tribhuj resistance imagery, philosophical moments.

### 5.3 Channel 3: News Article Snippets

**Format:** Text-heavy images that look like newspaper clippings, broadcast chyrons, or media GIFs from Bharatvarsh's alternate timeline. Designed to feel like real media from this world.

**Design types:** Newspaper front pages, broadcast screenshots, ticker overlays, small GIFs with text overlay.

**Caption style:** Medium. Contextualises the "media artifact" with audience-facing commentary. Bridges the in-world artifact and the real-world reader.

**Visual framework:**
- Newspaper masthead designs (Bharatvarsh national papers, state media)
- Broadcast chyron overlays (BVN-24x7 news ticker)
- Historical article layouts (yellowed paper, period typography for historical events)
- React-rendered text compositions with editorial layouts
- Feels like conventional media from this alternate timeline

**Best for:** Historical events (1717 divergence, 1978 emergency, 1984 ban), Novel Intro Items filter, breaking news from the 20-10 bombings, Tribhuj angle posts.

## 6. Operational Requirements

### 6.1 Website Integration
Every post must include `welcometobharatvarsh.com` — either embedded naturally in the narrative text or as a final footnote. Periodically suggest followers visit for deeper lore.

### 6.2 Cross-Channel Suggestions
From time to time, direct followers to other social platforms where different types of content appear. Build multi-platform habit.

### 6.3 Visual Production Rules
1. **Background patterns:** Designed via React rendering. These are the branded frames, overlays, and compositional elements.
2. **Key artwork:** AI-generated images in Jim Lee art style. These are the hero images — the centrepiece of each post.
3. **Jim Lee art style** is followed to the core for all visual direction and prompt design: dense linework, heavy blacks, dynamic compositions, cross-hatching for depth, cinematic framing, heroic proportions.
4. **Brand tokens:** All templates use Context B palette as CSS variables (Obsidian #0A0A0F, Navy #0B2742, Mustard Gold #F1C232, Powder Blue #E8F0FE, Cyan #00E5FF, Mesh Purple #8B5CF6).

### 6.4 Content Calendar Integration
This strategy governs how new rows are populated in `content-pipelines/bharatvarsh/calendar/content_calendar.csv`. Each row must specify:
- Which **story angle** it draws from (bharatsena / akakpen / tribhuj)
- Which **distillation filter** shapes it (living_without_religion / med_mil_progress / novel_intro)
- Which **content channel** formats it (declassified_report / graffiti_photo / news_article)

### 6.5 Long-Term Sequencing
Posts are not standalone — they form a narrative drip. When designing a batch:
- Vary angles across consecutive posts (don't do 3 Bharatsena posts in a row)
- Alternate between channels for visual variety
- Build reveals progressively (world → technology → characters → events → mystery)
- Seed questions early that later posts answer
- First 5 posts establish the world; subsequent batches introduce characters and conflict

### 6.6 Spoiler Safety
All content is spoiler-safe by default:
- Never reveal KACHA programme details beyond "classified military research"
- Never reveal Pratap's orchestration of the 20-10 bombings
- Never reveal Kahaan's final choice or power transfer
- Character introductions use dossier-style teases, never full arcs
- The central mystery ("who attacks a nation that watches everything?") is posed but never answered

### 6.7 Dashboard HITL Integration
Once the Content Pipeline dashboard is operational, this strategy feeds the human-in-the-loop sequence: the system proposes posts following these rules, the human reviews and approves, then the pipeline renders and schedules. Until then, this document is the manual governance layer.

## 7. Extensibility

### Adding New Filters
More distillation filters can be added as the content calendar evolves. Potential future filters:
- **Geopolitics & Global Standing** — Bharatvarsh's relationships with China, US, EU
- **Daily Life & Culture** — What ordinary citizens experience (Festive Fortnight, Candy-Bots, glide-car commutes)
- **The Underground** — Black markets, contraband, basement worship, resistance networks

### Adding New Channels
New content channel formats can be introduced:
- **Video / Reels** — Short atmospheric clips, broadcast hijack recreations
- **Interactive Stories** — Polls, choose-your-clearance-level threads
- **Audio Clips** — Intercepted transmissions, Mesh system audio logs

### Angle Evolution
As the campaign matures and the audience grows, the three angles can develop sub-angles:
- Bharatsena → Split into "Official Comms" vs "Leaked Internal" voices
- Akakpen → Split into "Elder Wisdom" vs "Young Warrior" perspectives
- Tribhuj → Split into "Old Guard Memory" vs "New Movement Action"
