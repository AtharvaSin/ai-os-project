# Bharatvarsh -- Visual & Art Direction Guide

> Canonical reference for all visual design decisions: color palettes, character appearances, uniforms, weapons, insignia, architecture aesthetics, and art direction rules.
> Use this document to maintain visual consistency across illustrations, image generation, comics, and marketing assets.

---

## I. Design Philosophy

Bharatvarsh's visual identity is **dark, cinematic, and grounded dystopian**. The world is beautiful but oppressive -- clean surfaces, institutional precision, selective lighting. Technology is present but restrained; it never screams sci-fi. The aesthetic language privileges **austerity as authority** and **minimalism as control**.

**Core tenets:**
- Dark backgrounds with selective, purposeful lighting
- Military precision in every uniform detail
- Tech glow effects are subtle (cyan/blue for Mesh systems, amber for Bharatsena)
- UI is translucent and illegible -- never readable dashboards
- Surveillance is "second-read" -- felt, not center-framed
- No bright/cheerful color palettes; no generic sci-fi tropes
- No light-mode aesthetics

## III. Character Visual Keys

- Medals: starts with 4, ends with 0

- Same palette and garment components; tailored shaping (clean, clipped lines)

**DO:**
- Default to powder-blue shirt + navy outer layer for HQ/office
- Show first-name-only identity on badges/greetings
- Use large back emblem, left small emblem, right division text on any coat with shoulder pads
- Turn on HUD monocle to signal combat/active deployment for augmented officers

**DON'T:**
- Add camouflage patterns, caps/helmets, or ribbons/medals not shown in canon
- Print surnames, mottos, or ornamental trim
- Place the emblem on the right shoulder pad
- Add slogans to any uniform surface

### Pulse Gun
- Standard patrol sidearm variant
- Clean regulation silhouette; matte, non-reflective finish
- No rails, gadgets, or aftermarket optics unless scene explicitly requires

### Dart (Akakpen Weapon)
- Sap-dart throwers: low-draw ballistas (10-15 m effective) using sleep saps
- Silent, precise aesthetic
- Red cap on triggers in peace hours

### Brace-Comm (Wrist Device)
- Military wrist band with clean casing
- Projects small holographic panes (call, map tile, payment confirm)
- Cyan/blue-white light; edges fade quickly; 30-50% opacity
- HQ: kept low-key (one tile at a time, auto-dismiss)
- Field: more dynamic during briefings
- Sound: sub-audible tap; never beeps in HQ

### HUD Monocle (Augmented Officers)
- Floating curved rectangular lens over one eye (Kahaan: left eye)
- Thin, translucent; electric-blue glow with tiny ticks/arcs
- Soft blue highlight on cheekbone/eyelid (keep subtle)
- OFF in office/HQ; ON in field operations
- Reflections: occasional glance-flares when pivoting

- Docking: stone cradle on desk; inductive charge; single white ember indicator
- Usage: tool, not spectacle -- controlled gestures only

### Giddh X-200 Scout Chopper

## VI. Army Insignia

### Logo Design
- **Core:** Large bold circle (Ashoka-style wheel) with 24 equally spaced radial spokes converging at center
- **Encircling rim:** Thick, bold stroke defining the wheel boundary
- **Triangular frame:** Downward-pointing equilateral triangle; apex at wheel bottom
- **Wings:** Two elongated isosceles triangles extending outward and slightly upward from the triangle's top edges, angling at ~45 degrees
- **Result:** Three-pronged structure -- one downward triangle beneath the wheel; two wing-like frames flanking the top

### Linework & Style
- Uniform bold black strokes; solid monotone (black-and-white)
- Wheel interior between spokes: white; spokes and rim: black
- Precisely symmetrical along the vertical axis
- White negative-space cutouts in triangle and wings

### Approved Placements
- **Back (large):** Centered shoulder-to-shoulder on coat/jacket
- **Left shoulder (small):** Reduced mark on left shoulder pad
- **Right shoulder:** NEVER -- reserved for text only
- **Plaques:** Lift lobby, press backdrop, conference wall
- **Door frosts:** War-room inner door micro-seal (10% opacity)

### Emblem Color Rules
- Mustard yellow on navy surfaces
- Navy blue on powder-blue surfaces
- Monochrome (black/white) for plaques and formal applications
- Never mixed; never reversed

### Faction Emblems

**Tribhuj Trident:**
- Geometric trident; each prong representing a different faith pointing to common purpose
- Graffiti form: found on slum walls as shorthand for hope and sedition
- **Dojo use:** Grey trident on orange cloth; hand-stitched; never corporatized
- New terrorists hijack the emblem in "slick neon geometry" -- a desecration of the original covenant

**Treaty Glyph (Akakpen):**
- Two nested mountain chevrons enclosing a hollow circle
- Proportions: outer chevron baseline = 1.00; inner = 0.66; circle diameter = 0.33 of chevron height
- Line weight: 1/18 of height
- Used on gate plaques, maps, sanctuary markers
- Never on dojo flags; never oversized

Civilians read prosperous, practical, modernized. Colors stay muted and earthy with one utilitarian accent. Fabrics trend khadi-tech (breathable weaves), ripstops, and recycled blends.

## VIII. Architecture & Environment Aesthetics

### Hypertech Cities -- Shared Visual DNA
- Glass-and-steel with vertical gardens on towers no one owns
- OxyPoles (tree-shaped catalytic air-scrub towers) at regular intervals: 8.5-9.2 m height; slender trunk; ringed vane crown 1.6 m outer diameter
- Glide-cars floating a few meters above asphalt on silent hover lanes with dashed beacons
- Algorithmic checkpoint towers: slim, 4.6 m teardrop profile masts; sensor heads; no visible weapons; read as "ambient architecture"
- Billboard-sized holograms for pollen counts and share prices (never garish advertising)
- Curb scanner bollards: 1100 mm height; frosted biometric window + matte QR panel; idle Control Blue halo
- Hover-lane beacons: 3.0 m pole with horizontal ring at 2.6 m; low-intensity blue
- Vertical-garden cassettes: modular S/M/L panels (600x900 to 1200x1800 mm); aluminum rails; drip irrigation; city-specific planting

### Indrapur HQ
- All-glass oval; pale grey interlayer; vertical fins at 3 m spacing
- Minimalist and austere; institutional cadence
- Interior: basalt desk (dark, satin), charcoal composite floors, frosted gradient glass partitions, graphite anodize metalwork

### The Wilds (Eastern & Himalayan)
- Natural materials: wood, stone, slate, wool, leather/rope, oxidized metals
- Hand-mended tech; no neon; no plastic sheen; no retroreflective films
- Carved timber signage (Deep Green base, letters cut 4-5 mm, in-painted off-white); prayer-wheel racks with hand-polish patina; slate notice boards
- Authority is communal, not institutional; surveillance is human sentries, not cameras
- Atmosphere: fog shafts through pines, frost glitter on rope fibers, prayer flags (stiff, not fluttery at altitude), hearth glow pooling against blue-cold exterior
- Seasonal wear: stone darkens 10-15% near base in winter; algae tints in monsoon; timber silvers on sun faces; rope fibers chalk with frost
- Treaty Glyph: two nested mountain chevrons enclosing hollow circle; cap-height scale on gate plaques; never oversized
- Dojo flags: Grey Trident on Orange (hand-stitched, matte); Treaty Glyph never appears on dojo fabrics

### Heritage Interface Rules (All Cities)
- Sacred plots meet smart-streets via green buffers (planter bands + shade trees)
- Signage reduces to two-line stack (EN + State) near sacred precincts
- No drones directly above worship roofs
- Algorithmic towers remain outside sacred perimeter by >=25 m
- Scanners (if any) sit on civic edge, never at the threshold
- Palette: base civic only in sacred plots; tricolor never as decor; festival overlays on temporary civic devices only (max 20%)
- Sound: natural voices/music dominate; reinforcement low and warm; exterior lighting 3000-3500 K, interior <=3000 K

## VIII. Art Direction Rules

### DO
- Use dark backgrounds with selective, purposeful lighting
- Maintain military precision in every uniform detail
- Keep tech glow effects subtle (cyan/blue for Mesh, mustard/amber for Bharatsena accents)
- Ground all technology in physical consequences (neural fatigue, tinnitus, dust, wear)
- Show honest material wear (stone darkens at drip lines, bronze goes umber at touch points)
- Use "bar reflections" across glass and faces for institutional mood
- Keep signage tri-lingual and contrast >= 4.5:1
- Show surveillance as ambient background, not focal spectacle

### DON'T
- No bright, cheerful color palettes
- No generic sci-fi tropes (no alien tech, no space, no force fields, no laser beams)
- No light-mode aesthetics
- No readable dashboard UIs (all screen content must be illegible glyphs)
- No chrome hardware in Indrapur HQ
- No brand logos on vehicles or equipment
- No camouflage patterns on standard uniforms
- No slogans or mottos on uniforms
- No emblem on right shoulder pad (text only)
- No extra medals/ribbons beyond what the story shows
- No strobe lights, siren arrays, or aggressive crowd-control visuals
- No tricolor used as textures or backgrounds (reserved for flags and ceremony)
- No religious text or icons on state hardware (civic neutrality)

### Lighting Principles
- Blue-hour is the canonical cinematic look (cool exterior, warm-neutral interior)
- Day presets are 5200-5600 K; Blue-hour exterior is 9000-10000 K
- War-room severity: use side-key and pane-raked bars across faces
- EMP-Safe/Low-Power: 3000-3200 K emergency strips only; deep shadows, silhouettes
- Wilds: lanterns at 2700-3000 K; natural fire; moonlight fill

### Camera & Composition
- **Authority shots:** 50-70 mm, neutral key + cool back
- **Severity shots (war-room):** 85 mm side-key; let panes rake bars across faces
- **Helipad/action:** 35 mm wides; top-light
- **Motorcade:** 50-70 mm tracking; keep wet-plaza reflections
- **Wilds council:** Warm interior pools against blue-cold exterior
- **Wilds dojo:** Rim light over staff rings; frost glitter; breath visible
