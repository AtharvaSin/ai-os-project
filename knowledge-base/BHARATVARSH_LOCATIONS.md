# Bharatvarsh -- Location Guide

> Canonical reference for every named location in the Bharatvarsh universe.
> Use this document to ensure geographic, architectural, and atmospheric consistency across prose, artwork, and marketing.

---

## Overview: The Three Administrative Blocs

Bharatvarsh is divided into three continental commands, each with a distinct geographic character, surveillance density, and cultural texture:

| Bloc | Geography | Surveillance Level | Key Function |
|------|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| Northern Plains Command | Vast alluvial plains, dense agriculture | Highest -- algorithmic checkpoints, AI towers, full Mesh | Population heartland, food production |
| Peninsular Command | Industrial coastline, tech plateaus, river deltas | High -- naval patrols, port security, metro surveillance | Industry, naval power, trade |
| Eastern & Himalayan Treaty Zone | Mountain ranges, forests, high-alpine passes | Lowest -- treaty-limited; no-fly valleys, tribal self-governance | Insurgent birthplace, alternative philosophies |

The capital functions are centered in **Indrapur**, the government seat within Northern Plains Command. Three hypertech metropoles -- Lakshmanpur, Mysuru, and Kolkata Corridor -- serve as the regime's showcase cities.

## I. Indrapur -- The Seat of Power

### Precinct & Setting
Government core of Indrapur, occupying the ceremonial axis of the old assembly grounds ("central government precinct"). This is where the Directorate governs from. General Pratap effectively lives at HQ.

### Headquarters Building

#### Massing & Exterior
- **Plan:** Low-rise, large-footprint oval; all-glass facade with deep mullion rhythm
- **Height:** ~12 storeys / ~52 m -- reads as "not very tall but commanding"
- **Facade:** Low-iron laminated glass with pale grey interlayer; vertical fins at 3 m spacing create a quiet, institutional cadence
- **Plaza:** Pale granite with micro-bevels; drainage slots align with facade grid
- **Aesthetic:** Minimal vertical-garden accents only at plaza level -- not on the tower. Keeps optics "austere"
- **Central core:** Sealed shaft (black design) running roof to basement to underground nuclear bunker, isolated from public shafts

#### Security Zoning (Building-Wide)

| Zone | Access | Areas |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Public** | Open | Plaza, entry hall, press gallery |
| **Controlled** | Badge | Upper admin floors, brief rooms, motorcade ramps |
| **Secure** | Badge + wrist-scan | Command level, war-room suite, roof apron |
| **Black** | Sealed protocol | Central shaft, bunker access, comms nodes |

#### Command Level (Top Floor)

The command level contains the key power spaces of the regime:

**Command Suite (Top-Floor Office)**
- West-biased for late-day backlight; skylight strip above
- **Tri-Anchor Wall** (centerline behind desk): foundational leader portrait relief (tone-on-tone metal), framed national preamble (matte off-white), strategic wall map (frosted glass overlay with low-chroma landmasses) -- all floating on a cold back-lit glass field
- **Desk:** Raised rostrum with dark basalt stone-slab top (satin finish, low specular)
- **Props:** Leather folio (blind-embossed emblem), matte pens, Omni-Handle dock (stone cradle)
- **Floor:** Charcoal acoustic composite, 10-12% reflectivity
- **Atmosphere:** Soft bar-like reflections across glass fields; institutional calm; authority through restraint

**War-Room / Situation Suite**
- Connected to Command Suite by a short glass corridor (not directly adjacent)
- **Centerpiece:** Elliptical glass map-table (2.6 x 1.6 m, 900 mm high) with internal cool LED ring
- **Perimeter:** Standing rails at compass points (graphite anodize, no chrome)
- **Screens:** Translucent command panes with illegible glyphs -- pale cyan/steel gradients, never readable dashboards
- **Light grammar:** Cool back-light through frosted partitions; soft bar reflections across faces and coats
- **Doors:** Two-stage (outer clear, inner frosted) with ceiling wrist-scan strip
- **Signage:** "WAR-ROOM / SITREP -- CLEARANCE RED" on inner door band

**Helipad & Evac Apron (Roof East)**
- Plain "H" marking only -- no numerics or alphas
- Safety-yellow chevrons along wind edge; anti-skid microtexture
- Flush beacon rings; fold-flat stretcher rack at apron edge
- Blast-baffled corridor from helipad door to war-room foyer (Red clearance)
- Transport: Twin-engine scout/transport capable of autonomous approach

**Motorcade Interfaces**
- Sub-grade motorcourt with weather canopy
- Dedicated VIP lift car rises to Top-Floor Security Vestibule
- **Escort formation (urban):** Lead scout drone -> two outrider hover-bikes -> state glide-car (sedan) -> tail security SUV -> overwatch drone at 25 m AGL
- Speed cap within precinct: 20 kph glide
- Blue-hour arrivals favored; reflective plaza stones give paint-hue play

#### Access Control
- Wrist-scan readers flush into corridor walls and ceiling fascias
- Ceiling strip (300 mm wide): idle cyan glow, turns white when scanning
- Wall insets at 1.2 m and 1.6 m heights (ambidextrous)
- No freestanding pedestals -- corridors kept clean for VIP traffic
- Threshold zones: Green (badge) -> Amber (badge + wrist-scan) -> Red (dual unlock + escort) -> Black (sealed shaft)

#### Lighting Presets

| Preset | CCT | Office Lux | War-Room Lux | Look |
|--------|-----|-----------|-------------|------|
| Day (Command Calm) | 5200-5600 K | 350-450 | 250-350 | Crisp, institutional |
| Blue-Hour (Theatre) | Ext 9000-10000 K / Int 4300-4600 K | 300 | 220-280 | High contrast, bars pronounced |
| EMP-Safe (Continuity) | 3000-3200 K strips only | 50-80 egress | 120-150 (table ring) | Deep shadows, silhouettes |

#### Materials & Finishes
- **Facade glass:** VLT 62-68%, neutral grey interlayer
- **Vertical fins:** Graphite anodize, matte
- **Desk top:** Dark basalt stone slab, satin (gloss ~10/100)
- **Floor:** Charcoal acoustic composite, 10-12% reflectance
- **Metalwork:** Brushed graphite anodize; fixings invisible
- **Accents:** None mandated -- neutrality preserves institutional tone

#### Signage System
- Tri-lingual: Latin (primary) / Devanagari (secondary) / State line (tertiary)
- Condensed geometric sans for Latin; square-terminal grotesk for Devanagari
- Monochrome spectrum only (black to mid grey to white)
- Emergency overlays in safety yellow; no red on this level
- Door IDs: "L12 -- DIRECTORATE HQ" in tri-lingual stack

#### Props (Always Present in HQ Scenes)
- **Command Suite:** Leather folio (blind-embossed emblem), two matte pens in shallow tray, Omni-Handle dock (stone cradle at desk right), Brace-Comm appearing only as single discreet tiles
- **War-Room:** Omni-Handle as primary interaction prop (pointer/baton modes); perimeter rails; thin illegible glyph streams on panes
- **Tri-Anchor Wall elements:** Leader portrait relief (tone-on-tone metal), framed preamble (matte off-white), strategic wall map (frosted glass, low-chroma landmasses)
- **Pratap's personal items:** Signet ring (brushed steel, army emblem engraved); Commander's Omni-Handle variant (pen-baton that telescopes, with concealed dagger mode)

#### Behavior & Etiquette (Recurring HQ Rules)
- Brace-Comm: single discreet cyan/white tiles; never clutter a frame
- HUD monocles: OFF within office/war-room unless explicitly story-critical; Pratap never wears one
- Right-shoulder law: "DIRECTORATE HQ" text block; emblem never on right
- Map-table usage: hands rest on outer 150 mm ring; inner field is projection only
- No freestanding pedestals in secure zones
- Motorcade speed cap: 20 kph glide within precinct

## II. Hypertech Metropoles

Three showcase cities demonstrate the regime's "engineered harmony." Each shares a common smart-street infrastructure but carries distinct geographic and climatic character.

### Shared Urban Infrastructure

**OxyPole v2 (Air-Scrub Tower)**
- Height: 8.5-9.2 m; slender trunk with ringed vane crown (1.6 m outer diameter)
- Spacing: 60-80 m on primary boulevards; 40-50 m on festival axes
- Night mode: Soft wash heads, subtle cyan status tick (<=2% area)
- Emergency/Curfew: Safety amber ring at base, 30 BPM
- Materials: Powder-coated aluminum (graphite), stainless composite crown, oxidized bronze base collar on ceremonial streets

**Algorithmic Checkpoint Towers**
- 4.6 m slim mast; teardrop profile (350 x 120 mm)
- Tri-camera cluster + lidar slit at 4.2 m; no visible weapons
- Curfew mode: Projected cone markers on pavement (blue-white)
- Reads as "ambient architecture" -- never sci-fi clutter

**Hover Lanes**
- Operational altitude band: 0-5 m
- Dashed lane beacons at 3 m intervals; service bay every 250-300 m
- Charging couplers and safety amber strobes at bays

**Metro Gate Banks**
- Standard bank: 6 lanes (4 standard, 1 accessible, 1 staff)
- Biometric + QR dual interface; throughput 35-40 ppl/min/lane
- Tri-script labels; tactile wayfinding nodes

**Street-Level Surveillance**
- Arterial boulevard checkpoints every 1.2-1.6 km
- Scanner halos (curb-integrated for pedestrians, upright hoops for automotive)
- Wrist-scan modules at thresholds (ceiling strips + wall insets)
- Hovercams: 12-20 m AGL non-festival; 8-12 m festival band
- Curfew: 21:00-06:00 within metropoles
- Color states: Green (pass) / Amber (additional scan) / Red (hold)
- "Polite inevitability" -- control tech ever-present but restrained

### Street-Level Surveillance & Crowd Control

The cities operate under a doctrine of "polite inevitability" -- control tech is ever-present but restrained. The visual grammar favors monochrome marks, clean glass, and matte metal.

**Checkpoint Typologies:**

| Type | Placement | Footprint | Hardware |
|------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Arterial Boulevard (Permanent) | Every 1.2-1.6 km on major boulevards, metro mouths | 24-30 m active zone | Curb-integrated scanner halos; ceiling wrist-scan strips; OxyPole with PA/camera |
| Plaza / Festival Surge (Modular) | Festival corridors, parade starts, stadium entries | 18-24 m modular | Mobile tripod halos; temporary crowd rails; hovercams at 8-12 m AGL |
| Side-Street Micro Gate (Pop-up) | Alley cut-throughs, school fronts, hospital perimeters | 8-12 m | Single upgraded OxyPole with scanner head, PA, camera nest |

**Flow Logic (Green / Amber / Red):**
- **Green (Pass):** Automotive drift <=20 kph through green chevrons; pedestrian walk-through with ceiling strip idle cyan; random wrist-scan on <=10% sample
- **Amber (Additional Scan):** Gentle arrow LED guides to halo bay; <=30 s dwell; roving plain-clothes officer glance
- **Red (Hold):** Lane light flips red; low modular rail extends; detention limited to repeat violations, curfew breach, or flagged ID
- **Fail-safe:** Equipment fault falls back to Green + manual spot-check only

**Curfew Regime:**
- Hours: 21:00-06:00 within metropoles
- Exemptions: Medical/emergency personnel, critical logistics, accredited press, registered night-shift workers (employer QR), diplomats, foreign tourists with curfew QR pass
- Signage: "CURFEW 21:00-06:00" tri-lingual with exemption pictos
- Night lighting: Guidance studs only; OxyPoles low glow; checkpoint towers standby

**Hovercam Protocol:**
- Non-festival: 12-20 m AGL
- Festival band: 8-12 m AGL
- Emergency drop: minimum 6 m AGL
- Spotlight: soft-edge beam, timed sweeps <=5 s; used for lost-child, medical, dispersal -- no face-dazzle

**Data & Privacy Optics:**
- Posted public: "Data retention: 30 days public -> purge" with appeal QR
- Implied (never posted): Archival persistence beyond 30 days in private repositories
- CCTV booth: Glassed, visible from concourses; custody clock shows days-to-purge (counts down from 30)

**Audio Policy:**
- All high-decibel signals banned in public control use (>78 dBA sustained)
- Default hierarchy: Chime > Voice (never sirens, whistles, air-horns)
- PA: One-way, brief, neutral register; 60-65 dBA at 1 m; <=5 s bursts

**Scene Beat Examples:**
- Spot-fine moment: a tap, a micro-tile glow, a white-to-cyan tick; no confrontation
- Dispersal: hovercam soft spotlight + chimes, not siren shoves
- Candy-bot guidance: LED "eyes" that track only when parent consents; issues candy tokens at pad kiosks

### Tri-Script Typography System (All Cities)

- **Latin (English):** Inter -- weights 400/600/800; variable axis for large-format signs
- **Devanagari (Hindi):** Noto Sans Devanagari -- 400/600/800
- **Telugu (Mysuru):** Noto Sans Telugu -- 400/600/800
- **Bengali (Kolkata):** Noto Sans Bengali -- 400/600/800
- **Stack order:** EN (top, largest) > Hindi (middle) > State line (bottom)
- **Grid:** 8-pt baseline grid for print/UI; 8-mm module for physical signs
- **Contrast:** >=4.5:1 for all text/background combinations
- **Day signs:** Dark text (#0B1D2A) on light panels (#F2F5F7)
- **Night signs:** Reversed, backlit (white text on #0B1D2A) with luminous accent bands
- **State emblem:** Top-right of government signage; safe area = emblem width all around; never scaled under 24 mm

### Lakshmanpur (Northern River-Plain Metropole)

#### Geography & Climate
- Broad alluvial plain; meandering river with levees and shallow flood terraces
- Low relief; dense inner city giving way to regimented new sectors
- **Winter (Dec-Jan):** Cool and fog-prone mornings; low contrast; OxyPoles ghost in haze
- **Pre-Monsoon (Apr-May):** Hot, dusty "brass heat"; glare off pale stone; heat shimmer above hover lanes
- **Monsoon (Jun-Sep):** Heavy rain; mirror asphalt; saturated greens on vertical gardens; river swells
- **Post-Monsoon (Oct-Nov):** Clearer skies; fresh vegetation; evening air crisp

#### Architecture & Materials
- Pale limestone/sandstone; pale concrete; oxidized bronze rails; ceramic jali panels
- Fog reduces apparent saturation -- lean into texture (micro-pitting, efflorescence)
- Tall columnar street trees for sightline continuity
- Mist-friendly matte signage; sandstone accents on ceremonial streets

#### Signature Weather Vignettes
- **"Milk-glass morning":** Human silhouettes through fog; muted banners; OxyPole halos
- **"Brass noon":** Wavering road markings, mirage ripples at 20-30 m
- **"Monsoon mirror night":** Puddled neon; hovercam cones reflected; raindrop bokeh

#### Heritage Micro-Sets
- **Hindu:** Compact sandstone mandir with single shikhara; shallow forecourt water-mirror
- **Muslim:** Three-bay prayer hall; twin low minarets; geometric jali
- **Christian:** Basilica plan with short bell tower; pale stone ribs; quiet side garden

#### Street Section
- **Ceremonial boulevard:** 41-45 m ROW (6 vehicular lane equivalents, hover band above median); 5-6 m sidewalks; median planting with OxyPole spacing 60-80 m
- **Neighborhood spine:** 24-28 m ROW; OxyPole spacing 80-100 m; checkpoint tower at intersections only

#### Commerce & Crowd
- Civilian streetwear: sleek, breathable fabrics; small holo-badges; pragmatic footwear
- Social axis: "military-employee vs civilian" rather than class-by-wealth; uniforms and ID bands read clearly against civic palette
- Commerce skins: more sandstone/bronze accents; fog-friendly matte signage

#### Everyday Texture
Morning commutes in silent glide-cars, biometric turnstiles instead of ticket counters, billboard-sized holograms proclaiming "Working Hard, So You Don't Have To." Festival Fortnight -- the single state-sponsored holiday -- has just ended, but neon drones still patrol for curfew violators. In cafes, civilians joke that paying cash gets you stared at "as if money were a museum piece." Street food aromas compete with ozone from portable drone chargers. Children swap strategy-game cheat codes from after-school soldier academies while grandparents stream censored retrospectives of pre-ban Bollywood on courtyard projectors. Bus passengers complain that "army men reserve all the special perks for themselves."

#### Canonical Scene: Curfew Sweep (Monsoon)
Rain at 10-12 degree angle; reflective asphalt; shuttered chai kiosks; checkpoint tower projects cone markers; hover lane empty. Sodium pools at 3700 K mixed with holo spill at 6500 K; specular streaks; puddle caustics. Signage reads "CURFEW IN EFFECT 21:00-06:00" in tri-script.

### Mysuru (Interior Plateau Tech-Hub)

#### Geography & Climate
- Slight elevation; granitic shelves; dry winters with pronounced wet season
- Crisp horizons; stronger sun angles
- **Dry Season (Dec-Mar):** Clear air, hard shadows; stone warms quickly
- **Pre-Monsoon (Apr-Jun):** Heat build-up; dust in late afternoons; vegetation stressed
- **Monsoon (Jul-Sep):** Sudden lushness; heavy sheets of rain; runoff rills off terraces
- **Shoulder (Oct-Nov):** Pleasant, balanced light; good for civic ceremonies

#### Architecture & Materials
- Granite, basalt plinths, laterite blocks; fiber-cement louvers
- Matte ceramic in plazas (no specular glare during dry months)
- Pergola-shaded transit forecourts; xeric shrubs and hardy lawn bands
- Stone-forward storefronts with dark contrasts

#### Signature Weather Vignettes
- **"Granite glare noon":** Bold shadow geometry; hover lanes read as crisp ribbons
- **"Monsoon terrace sheet":** Rain cascading from step-eaves; water chains beating in courtyards

#### Street Section
- Tech-park terraces stepping down granite shelves; pergola-shaded glide-car pads
- Basalt plinth street edges; hover-lane chevrons as basalt inlays
- Street section: 24-28 m ROW; OxyPoles align with pergola posts

#### Heritage Micro-Sets
- **Hindu (Terrace Mandir):** Gopuram-inspired gateway simplified into parametric fins; stepped court; granite/laterite plinth; deep shadows at noon through fin faces; monsoon sends sheets down the fins
- **Muslim (Arcade Masjid):** Deep arcade for shade; low domes; granite plinth; facade rhythm from thick columns casting sharp noon shadows; cool ceramic floor
- **Christian (Hillside Chapel):** Hill-slope chapel in granite; single bell arch; windward porch; golden hour gives long crisp shadows; monsoon turns steps into reflective bands

#### Canonical Scene: Granite Glare Noon
Empty terrace street; slim checkpoint tower; hover-beacons idle; flag base capped; dust in slant light. 5000 K golden-hour; strong shadow bars. Minimalism and long shadows; quiet, watchful order.

#### Commerce
- Stone-forward storefronts; pergolas; dark stone contrasts
- Dust-mesh covers on scanners during dry season
- Festival overlay (Y26): Monsoon Teal + Copper Leaf trims on civic stalls only; sacred facades stay overlay-free

### Kolkata Corridor (Deltaic Aerotropolis)

#### Geography & Climate
- Lowland delta; embankments; mangrove influence; very humid summers
- Frequent pre-monsoon evening squalls (nor'westers)
- **Pre-Monsoon (Mar-May):** Humid, storm-prone evenings; turbulent clouds; sodium pools + distant lightning
- **Monsoon (Jun-Sep):** Long, wind-driven rain; flooded curb edges; soft, low-contrast days
- **Winter (Dec-Jan):** Brief, mild; clearer nights
- **Post-Monsoon (Oct-Nov):** Sparkling air after downpours; algae sheens on stone near water

#### Architecture & Materials
- Corrosion-aware metals; anti-slip terracotta tiles; sealed concrete for salt-air
- Deep balconies with rain-chains (40 mm cups, anti-mosquito siphons)
- Covered skywalks ensure dry access; air curtains at portals
- Flood line markers at +250 mm from pavement

#### Signature Weather Vignettes
- **"Nor'wester street":** Banners whipping, raindrops at oblique, reflections streaked by wind
- **"Blue-hour levee":** Puddled embankments, long sodium reflections, hover berths pulled back from waterline

#### Street Section
- Embankment road: 18-24 m ROW; flood step on river side (+250 mm); hover berths offset
- Covered skywalks crossing above; stilted service blocks
- Anti-slip terracotta at crossings; flood line markers on civic pylons

#### Heritage Micro-Sets
- **Hindu (Delta Mandir):** Terracotta-heavy curved rooflines; patterned brickwork; +300 mm flood plinth; rain-chains with anti-mosquito siphons; terracotta glows under lanterns at evening
- **Muslim (Eaved Masjid):** Broad eaves; curved cornices; river-breeze verandahs; ablution cistern under canopy; moisture-resistant plaster; oversize downpipes hidden in column cores
- **Christian (Riverside Basilica):** Screened verandah facing river breeze; long nave; salt-aware metals; anti-condensation edge seals on glazing; blue-hour gloss on long interior light pools

#### Canonical Scene: Nor'wester Street
Street perpendicular to water; banner truss flexing; kiosks lashed; palms bent; puddles streaked by crosswind. Sodium at 3600-3800 K + cyan holo spill; lightning rim hits clouds. Energy in the air, not apocalypse.

#### Commerce
- Kiosks on leeward side during nor'westers; lash points integral to kiosk feet
- Anti-corrosion trims; sealed edges; canopy drainage visible
- Festival overlay (Y29): Delta Cyan + Marigold Gold trims on banners only

## III. Peninsular Command (Overview)

The industrial and naval heart of Bharatvarsh. While less detailed in the source novel than the Northern Plains or Treaty Zone, the Peninsular Command encompasses:

- **Industrial coastline:** Heavy manufacturing, naval shipyards, defense R&D complexes
- **Tech plateaus (Mysuru region):** Software, optics, advanced materials
- **River deltas (Kolkata Corridor):** Trade, aerotropolis logistics, flood-managed infrastructure
- **Naval power:** Port security, maritime patrols, trade route enforcement
- **Surveillance:** High -- comparable to Northern Plains in urban centers; port/harbor-specific security layers

### Peninsular Signage
- Follows the tri-script system: EN > HI > State line (Telugu for Mysuru region, Bengali for Kolkata Corridor)
- Naval installations use "PENINSULAR COMMAND" on right-shoulder text blocks and gateway plaques
- Port/harbor signage adds maritime pictograms to the standard set

### Key Functions
- Hosts the bio-medical complex: anti-ageing serums, memory-editing chips, advanced prostheses
- Consumer tech R&D before de-classification to civilian firms
- Solar-powered desert laboratories (border regions) for defense cybernetics
- Major trade partner interface: EU, Japan, US tech co-development

## IV. Northern Plains Command

### DNA & Atmosphere
Vast, geometric agriculture; orderly grids; spotless 4x4-lane expressways; polite-but-ever-present state optics. This is the bloc where "obedience feels normal." The world that works "too well" -- roads whisper, kiosks smile, posters thank you for being good.

### Highway & Control Stack
- **Carriageway:** 4 lanes per direction (8 total) with vegetated median (3-5 m)
- **OxyPoles:** Every 200 m along median; electrical poles every 50 m offset
- **AI Traffic Towers ("Sutradhar"):** Every ~3 km on expressways; 12.5 m triangular mast; 3x gimballed 4K cams + lidar ring; single white beacon pulse for hazard
- **Night rule:** No default curfew on the Plains; lighting drops to guidance studs only; OxyPoles on low glow; towers in standby
- **Highway patrol cadence:** Every ~15 km near towns; ~50 km across empty inter-city spans

### Algorithmic Checkpoints
- **Arch-scanner:** Clear height 4.8 m; graphite anodize; frosted emitters
- **State transitions:** GREEN -> AMBER -> RED via 300 ms cross-fades; never strobe
- **Retractable bollards:** 210 mm diameter; rise/fall in 1.2 s (normal) / 0.7 s (emergency)
- **Signage (bilingual):** Hindi primary "हरित -- पार करें" / English "GREEN -- PROCEED"
- **Behavior:** Randomized scheduling ("algorithmic intervals"); fail-safe = bollards auto-drop

### Agrarian Landscape
- **Primary geometry:** Center-pivot irrigation circles (200 m / 400 m radius)
- **Seasonal palette:** Mustard bloom (acid yellow ribbons) + wheat heads (gold-tan seas) as primary read; sugarcane walls (deep mid-green) as windbreak bands
- **Secondary:** Drip-grid rectangles near town perimeters; vertical-stack paddy near metro edges (background only)

### Service Stops (Propaganda-Heavy, "Friendly")
Recurring sequence (order varies): Pledge Corner -> School Exhibit -> Memorial Wall

- **Pledge Corner:** Low dais, hand-scan plinth, tri-lingual credo; backdrop LED ribbon (muted)
  - "Order Feeds All / अनुशासन से समृद्धि" (discipline -> abundance)
- **School Exhibit:** Rotating STEM diorama (precision farming, drones, OxyPole science)
- **Memorial Wall:** Local martyrs & disaster responders; monochrome names; QR for stories
- **Amenities:** EV fast-charge bays; water + nutrient stations; biometric pay kiosks

**Slogan families (canon tone: "peace-through-strength," benevolent optics):**
- "Order Feeds All / अनुशासन से समृद्धि"
- "Work. Rest. Rise. / काम. आराम. उन्नति."
- "Together We Keep the Peace / हम सब शांति निभाते हैं"
- Placement strictly on civic surfaces; never on uniforms

### Township Architecture
- **Prosperity read:** Advanced, robust -- white micro-cement and pale stone, solar pergolas, rainwater buttresses, roof agro-beds
- **Civics cluster:** Clinic (sometimes in repurposed shrine footprint), panchayat/admin block, mandi bay with parallel freight line siding
- **Workwear:** Modernized, functional khadi-tech blends; modular pockets; breathable coatings; reflective trims
- **Language:** Hindi primary on public signage; English sublines; regional tongues persist locally
- **Command text:** "NORTHERN PLAINS COMMAND" on right-shoulder blocks and gateway plaques

### Vehicles

## VII. Design Rules Across All Locations

### Universal Visual Grammar
1. **Surveillance as second-read:** Check towers slim and quiet; drones in bokeh or secondary read; enforcement energy felt, not center-framed
2. **Tech minimalism:** UI translucent and illegible; Brace-Comm one tile at a time; never wallpaper a frame with dashboards
3. **No generic sci-fi:** No alien tech, no space elements, no force fields; technology grounded in physicality
4. **Right-shoulder law:** All Bharatsena uniforms carry TEXT-ONLY on right shoulder (bloc/command name); emblem never on right
5. **Tri-lingual signage:** Cities use EN > HI > State; Wilds use Tribal > EN; always maintain contrast >= 4.5:1
6. **Heritage respect zones:** No drones above worship roofs; scanners outside sacred perimeters by >= 25 m
7. **Color discipline:** Base civic palette dominates; festival overlays never exceed 30% of frame; tricolor reserved for flags and ceremony only

### City vs. Wilds Visual Contrast

| Element | City | Wilds |
|---------|------|-------|
| Materials | Glass, anodized aluminum, ceramic, concrete | Wood, stone, slate, wool, oxidized metals |
| Light sources | LED, hologram, translucent panes | Lanterns, hearth fire, natural light |
| Signage | Backlit, tri-script, 8-mm grid | Carved/painted, Tribal+EN, matte |
| Sound | Chimes, PA (rare), hover-car hum | Wind, rope creak, prayer wheels, bow-snap |
| Authority | Institutional, impersonal | Communal, personal |
| Surveillance | Total, ambient | Minimal, human sentries |
| Color identity | Navy/graphite/cyan | Deep greens/marigold/vermilion |
