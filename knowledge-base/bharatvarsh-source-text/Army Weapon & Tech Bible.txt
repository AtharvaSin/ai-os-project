Bharatsena Armory & Tech — Visual Bible

Use these sections as “drop-in” spec sheets when describing soldiers, their gear, and scenes. Unless a variant is explicitly listed, stick to the default (canon) look.

0) Uniform touch-points (for placement consistency)

Palette: navy outer layers, powder-blue shirts.

Logo colors: mustard yellow on navy, navy on powder-blue.

Logo placement: large mark centered on the back; small mark on left shoulder pad; right shoulder pad = division/command text only (ALL CAPS; no logo).

1) Sidearms (standard pistols)

1A. Visual language

Type: compact service pistols (clean, regulation silhouettes).

Carry: twin high-riding shoulder holsters under/over coat depending on office vs field.

Finish: matte, non-reflective; minimal ornament.

Controls: simple slide serrations; low-profile iron sights (no laser dots by default).

1B. Pose & staging cues

Office/HQ: holsters mostly concealed by the open navy coat; grips just visible at ribs.

Field: holsters exposed; one pistol frequently drawn, with two others floating near shoulder level around Kahaan (see §3 for the control tech).

1C. Prompt kit (drop-in)

“Compact matte-black service pistol; low-profile sights; crisp slide; no accessories. Holstered high along the ribs (twin shoulder rig). One pistol drawn; stance squared and steady.”

2) Holster system — “Mag-Holster” plates (standard issue)

2A. Visual language

Mount: slim magnetic plates integrated into the shoulder rig/under-coat webbing.

Read: almost invisible under the officer’s open coat; gives the impression of frictionless lock/unlock when drawing or stowing pistols.

Motion cue: tiny metallic “settle” when a holstered pistol snaps home.

2B. Prompt kit

“Slim magnetic holster plates hugging the rib line; pistols click into place with a frictionless, magnet-lock motion; nearly invisible under the open navy coat.”

(Visual analogy: consumer magnetic gun mounts/holsters exist; cite for plausibility only, not canon.) (JM4 Tactical Holsters, The Stinger Tools, UnderTech UnderCover)

3) Remote weapon control — “float” behavior (Kahaan’s signature)

3A. Visual language

Effect: two service pistols hover near Kahaan at shoulder height, subtly tilting as if on invisible gimbals.

Control cue: a faint electric-blue shimmer line between Kahaan and the floating guns (minimal, tasteful), echoing his HUD tone.

Discipline: never clutter with holograms; keep the effect understated and precise.

3B. Prompt kit

“Two compact pistols floating at shoulder height with a faint electric-blue control shimmer; micro-adjusting in the air as if magnetically gimballed; no sci-fi noise or excessive UI.”

4) HUD Monocle (augmented officers; Kahaan)

4A. Visual language

Form: a floating, curved rectangular lens in front of one eye (Kahaan: left eye), thin and translucent.

Color: electric blue glow; tiny HUD ticks/glyphs, never legible text.

Reflections: soft blue highlight on cheekbone/eyelid; keep it subtle.

4B. Behavior

Office: powered down or quiescent (barely visible).

Field: active—micro-animations in the lens; occasional glance-flares when he pivots.

4C. Prompt kit

“Single electric-blue HUD monocle—thin, floating lens over the left eye; translucent with faint ticks and arcs; soft blue reflections on the cheekbone; minimal, surgical UI.”

(Visual analogy: tiny clip-on AR HUD ‘monocle’ devices exist in the real world.) (Wearable Technologies, Hackster, Medium)

5) Brace-Comm (wrist device)

5A. Visual language

Form: a military wrist band with clean casing; projects small holographic cards (call pane, map tile, payment confirm).

Projection: compact 3D panes hovering inches above the wrist; cyan/blue-white light; edges fade quickly.

Etiquette: kept low-key in HQ; more dynamic in field briefings.

5B. Prompt kit

“Military Brace-Comm wrist band; crisp 3D holo panes (call/map/payment) floating just above the wrist—clean cyan/blue-white light, fast falloff, no clutter; officer gestures to dismiss.”

(Visual analogy: research and patents for wrist-worn holographic projection/mid-air displays exist.) (Google Patents, Columbia University Computer Science, Chalmers Publication Library (CPL))

6) Long guns (only when scene requires)

The novel centers visuals on sidearms; long guns appear rarely and should remain generic unless a chapter specifies otherwise.

6A. Visual language (generic)

Type: standard patrol carbines.

Look: matte; no rails or gadgets; sling-carried; keep shapes readable and unbranded.

6B. Prompt kit

“Plain patrol carbine, matte finish, slung low; no accessories, no rails; regulation silhouette.”

7) Non-lethal & field medical

7A. Field braces / supports

Seen post-engagement (Kahaan, Manan): shoulder support, neck braces, straps—functional, minimal, hospital-issue white/neutral.

7B. Prompt kit

“Clean medical braces (shoulder/neck), fabric straps and buckles; white/neutral materials; no branding.”

8) Command & battlespace visuals (HQ / war room)

8A. Screens & maps

Look: translucent panels, abstract glyphs/graphs; text illegible by design.

Light: cold back-light; reflections cue duty/bar motif on glass walls.

8B. Prompt kit

“Translucent command panels with abstract glyphs/graphs; cold back-light; reflections cast soft ‘bars of duty’ across glass.”

9) Kahaan’s Bike (include when scenes call for mobility)

Canon establishes that Kahaan operates with speed and precision; the bike reinforces that visual. Keep the description tactical and clean, with no brand names.

9A. Visual language

Silhouette: scout-class combat motorcycle—lean, minimal bodywork; upright stance; medium knobbies for mixed terrain.

Finish: matte navy/graphite with mustard emblem stenciled small on the tail or side panel (if on powder-blue paneling, switch emblem to navy).

Lighting: slit-style headlamp; optional blackout cover for stealth.

Mounts: low-profile pannier hardpoints (usually empty), under-seat tool roll; no ostentatious racks.

Sync: the Brace-Comm can flash a tiny blue status icon in his HUD when the bike acknowledges commands (keep cues minimal).

9B. Optional variants to match scene tone (choose one)

Urban pursuit: road-biased tires; slightly lower bars; tighter turning posture.

Rural/rough terrain: higher ride height; bash plate hint; dust veil off the rear.

Stealth patrol: de-emphasized exhaust note; headlamp off; darker paint.

(Visual analogy: special-operations and military scout/stealth motorcycles are real; e.g., modern electric SOF bikes used for silent approach. Use only as mood reference, not as a brand or spec.) (WIRED)

9C. Prompt kit

“Scout-class combat motorcycle—lean, minimal bodywork; matte navy/graphite; slit headlamp; medium knobbies. Small army emblem stenciled on tail (mustard on navy / navy on powder-blue). Low-profile pannier hardpoints, usually empty. Dust trails over an empty battlefield; bike cues Kahaan’s HUD with a tiny blue status flicker.”

10) Division/command text (right shoulder only)

Use an existing bloc exactly (ALL CAPS): NORTHERN PLAINS COMMAND, PENINSULAR COMMAND, or EASTERN & HIMALAYAN TREATY ZONE.

Type is plain military block; no slogans, no quotes.

11) Scene assembly recipes (ready-to-use)

A) “Office brief / contained threat”

Powder-blue shirt + open navy coat; twin holsters mostly hidden.

Monocle off (glass faint); Brace-Comm shows a small call tile.

Large back emblem (mustard on navy); small left-shoulder emblem; right shoulder pad shows bloc text.

One pistol discreetly checked under the coat hem.

B) “Active field contact (Kahaan focal)”

Coat open or shed; holsters exposed; one pistol drawn; two float near shoulders with a faint blue shimmer.

Monocle active (electric blue); tiny UI ticks only.

Dusty empty battleground; spent casings; low sun rim light.

Bike staged in background (three-quarter view), matte navy/graphite; emblem stencil small on tail.

C) “Aftermath / evac”

Weapons holstered; Brace-Comm projects a small blue-white medevac tile.

Medical braces visible on injured; stretcher edge in frame.

Background: rooftop infirmary or helipad wash; keep tech minimal and clean.

12) Strict “Do / Don’t” (to keep everything on-model)

Do

Keep weapons unbranded, clean, and regulation.

Use mustard-on-navy / navy-on-powder-blue for all emblems.

Reserve the right shoulder for text only (no emblem).

Keep HUD/Brace-Comm UI minimal and illegible.

Don’t

No camouflage patterns, rails, lasers, or aftermarket optics unless a scene explicitly requires it.

No slogans, mottos, or quotes on uniforms.

No extra medals/ribbons beyond what the story shows.

No visible serials or real-world logos.
