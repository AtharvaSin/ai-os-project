Major Kahaan Arshad – a Complete Character Bible

The following narrative profile is organised according to the “outside-in” method of character-bible building—moving from demographic facts through history, psychology, relationships, and finally dramatic arc—an approach recommended by professional craft guides on creating story or series bibles. 1. Face and First Impressions

Kahaan Arshad is twenty-six: lean-muscled, quick-footed, always turned out with regimental precision. In day uniform he favours a powder-blue shirt beneath an open navy over-coat whose twin holsters cradle the famous flying pistols the weapons-lab built for him . During field operations a single electric-blue HUD monocle flashes across one eye—a visual reminder that part of his brain is threaded with neural diodes that let him magnetically steer compact firearms and pilot light vehicles by thought. Those same diodes leave a faint surgical scallop behind his right ear and gift him migraines, tinnitus and transient paralysis if he over-exerts them . Off-duty his clothes turn earth-toned and practical; Kahaan’s need to blend is as strong as his need to dominate.

2. Origins: the Soldier Shaped by Loss

He was born in 1999 in a Delhi barracks hospital, an only child who lost his mother, Aaliyah Khan Arshad, to leukaemia before he could form more than a handful of tactile memories. That absence wired a permanent, almost frantic hunger for significance into him. His father, Dr Sahil “Omar” Arshad, poured his grief into work and his hopes into the boy—brokering early introductions to cadet-school mentors, feeding him a steady diet of nationalist hero-tales, and eventually overseeing the diode-implant trial that would save Kahaan’s life after Africa and bind him ever tighter to the military-industrial machine .

Kahaan’s file is littered with precocious milestones: top-of-class at the academy, a début mission in Congo that won him his first Purple Heart for extracting twelve men from a rebel-held town , instant battlefield legend status, and a rapid climb to the rank of major at an age that provokes both admiration and envy among the ranks.

3. The Africa Fracture

Everything that follows bends around the peace-keeping fiasco in eastern Africa. Kahaan, leading a relief detail, tried to body-shield a comrade from a burqa-clad suicide bomber. The blast shredded flesh and future in equal measure; battlefield surgeons grafted in the diode lattice as a last resort. The tech turned him into a living weapons-platform—yet it also sowed chronic PTSD. The smell of burning circuitry or an echoing energy blast can still jerk him back into the smoke and sand of that street, paralysing him mid-conversation . From that day onward the army Prince carried a private fear: What if the institution I bleed for grinds me into a disposable pawn?

4. Physical and Technical Competence

Core talents – elite marksman; squad-level tactician; fluent drone & sensor analyst (his dissection of Phoenix Mall CCTV is textbook) .

Augmented skills – thought-guided “Mag-Holster” pistols and the ability to spin up a Giddh-X scout-chopper hands-free, provided the craft’s circuitry is tuned to his implant frequency .

Vulnerabilities – implant circuits break under EMP or heavy concussion, stranding him with an ordinary body and ringing eardrums; over-use triggers nose-bleeds, shakes, and black-outs.

5. Psychological Machinery

Dominant traits – incandescent ambition, charismatic command presence, analytic speed, sardonic humour.

Primary motivation – to etch his name into history as the youngest Supreme Commander of Bharatvarsh; the hunger for a legacy outlives every briefing and bullet.

Secondary fear – obscurity: becoming “just another piece” on the general’s board.

Ethical stance (Act I) – “Ends first, ethics later—so long as the long-term optics survive.” He will bend rules, stage raids on treaty land, even leverage hostages if it forwards the mission .

Catalyst for change – discovering that the very army he worships is breeding cyborg suicide-bombers in Facility IV, masterminded by his own father . The revelation detonates his moral compass and forces a re-alignment from statist zealot to reluctant humanist.

Final credo (Act IV) – “Power concentrated corrupts; leadership worthy of loyalty must be given back to the led.”

6. Emotional Weather & Contradictions

Kahaan’s swagger is wallpaper over fractures: routine nightmares, a lingering survivor’s guilt, and the lonely cold of a man who treats romance as off-duty recreation—one-night stands that end before breakfast, never a relationship that could threaten the timetable of command. When Hana, his closest comrade, needles him about yet another honey-trap incident, her words land hard precisely because they touch this hollow centre .

7. Web of Relationships

8. Public Image and Symbolism

To the citizenry Kahaan is “the Military Prince”—poster child of the regime, bearer of two Purple Hearts, the major who never cracks under fire . His flying guns circling at shoulder-height have become a hologram-banner in recruitment ads. Yet those same weapons symbolise the fine thread that tethers him to institutional control: a literal extension of the state’s engineering inside his skull.

Inside his over-coat he has stitched the scorched squad patch he tore from a Congo uniform, a private reliquary reminding him who paid the price for his medals.

9. Ideological Trajectory

Act I – Loyalist Riser
Kahaan embraces the army manifesto that argues democracy has corroded; only a martial hand keeps chaos at bay .

Act II – Fracture
The discovery of black-ops bio-wing corpses and the father’s confession of state-manufactured terror shatter that certainty .

Act III – False Accord
Briefly he flirts with the generals’ vision of a utopian military decade—immortality, predictive oracles, the “necessary” purge —only to watch Manan’s ruined spine and the civilian death-toll contradict every promise.

Act IV – Break & Gift
In the climax he refuses Pratap’s offer of succession, exposes the cyborg programme to the world press, and relinquishes command credentials, choosing civic accountability over personal supremacy (novel finale outline ).

10. Residual Mystery and Story Seeds

Romantic Future – The novel ends without a love-plot, but Hana’s unspoken loyalty and Kahaan’s first steps toward vulnerability leave space for slow-burn intimacy—or a painful ideological rift—in sequels.

Diode Degradation – Engineers warned the lattice has a finite half-life. Will Kahaan one day face total neural failure as karmic debt for past power?

Public Trial – Having testified against Pratap, does “the Prince” become folk-hero reformer or convenient scapegoat when the regime reels?

11. Voice and Dialogue Notes for Writers

Kahaan’s speech pattern tilts crisp-military with knife-edge sarcasm. Under extreme stress (PTSD flash, implant overload) his sentences fragment into clipped combat shorthand. Post-revelation humility should soften—but not erase—the native precision; readers must hear the same mind wrestling new ideals.

12. Log-Line (Quick Reference)

A decorated, cyber-enhanced major races to quash a terror plot that could crown his career—until he uncovers his own army’s complicity and must decide between the legacy he craves and the humanity he once vowed to protect.

Kahaan Arshad now stands on the page as a three-dimensional figure: scarred child of bereavement, glittering product of a militarised nation, living weapon haunted by the blast that birthed him, and—ultimately—a man who learns that real command lies not in hovering guns or ranks on a collar, but in the courage to set both down.
