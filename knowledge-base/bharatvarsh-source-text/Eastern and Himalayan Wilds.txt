C2) Band-by-Band environment behavior

Three altitude bands govern color/contrast, moisture, and edge wear.

Foothill Green (≈ 900–1,600 m)

Palette feel. Warmer stones, mossy seams, terraced soil; greens richer, contrast moderate.

Surface behavior. Morning fog dew; algae on stone steps; timber silvering on sun faces.

Typical vignettes. Fog shafts through pines; prayer wheels with hand-polish rings; mule shoes scrape sparks on granite at night.

Cold Spruce (≈ 1,600–2,800 m)

Palette feel. Cooler neutrals, deodar shadow bands; higher contrast; dust in dry months.

Surface behavior. Wind scoring on slate edges; rope fibers chalk with frost; bronze handles warm up with touch.

Typical vignettes. Switchbacks; caltrops flagged with marigold dots; silent lanes on ridges.

High Alpine (≈ 2,800–4,200 m)

Palette feel. Blue-grey rocks; sparse greens; snow/ice dominate micro-spec.

Surface behavior. Crusted snow (not powder) holds boot edges; matte ice sits like glass with micro-pitting.

Typical vignettes. Pikas skitter; breath plumes; flags stiff, not fluttery; sound becomes small.
