Phase 3 — Psychology + Motion Grammar (ready to use)

A) Psychology model

Core wound: institutional betrayal by command.

Surface goal: punish betrayers and expose Operation KACHA.

Real need: regain agency without becoming what he hunts.

Moral rails: civilians off-limits; precision violence only; no spectacle.

Values under stress: truth over chain of command; teammates over mission optics.

Blind spots: reflex distrust of uniforms; over-rotation to “solo” when trust wobbles.

Triggers: biometric chokepoints; handler cadence; sublevel signage; cuffs/collars.

Stress tells: jaw set, lip wet, micro head-tilt before breach; right hand checks mag base.

Decompression: weapon strip, dry-fire cadence 3×3×3, boots unlaced, five slow Telugu breaths.

Recovery rituals: palm press on doorframe before sleep; kit laid in L-shape within arm’s reach.

Hard lines: no civilian body count; no planted evidence; no torture.

Redemption lever: choosing exposure over execution when presented clear proof.

B) Voice, interaction, interrogation

Speech: clipped declaratives. Minimal qualifiers.

Diction: Hyderabad Telugu flavor. Allowed inserts for cadence only: “ekkada?”, “namaste rām,” “aithe,” “sāru,” “inka,” “chalu.”

Interrogation method: silence first, then single-lane prompts. Avoids threats; uses facts + timing.

Radio brevity: time, action, place. Example: “Two up. Left clear. Ladder down.”

C) Disable/kill decision tree (operational ROE)

Identify non-combatants → disengage or redirect flow.

Hostile armed?

No → verbal check → flex-cuff if obstructive.

Yes → cover available?

Yes → non-lethal first: Akakpen dart, throw-light, shoulder lock, zip-tie.

No → flash→two-step angle→center-mass stop with suppressed sidearm or pulse-gun analog.

Multiple hostiles or handler present → escalate to decisive fire, then immediate exfil.

Medical check on downed civilians or allies before movement.

Always clear for cameras and comms sniffers before leaving.

D) Motion grammar (global rules)

Economy: no extra steps; no flourish.

Stance: narrow athletic; elbows kept in; muzzle never crosses friendly lanes.

Transitions: low light = heel-toe; open asphalt = forefoot roll; ladders = three points.

Signature control: uses ambient noise spikes, grid flickers, and crowd pulses to mask motion.

Breach rhythm: breathe→count 3→hinge or charge→slice→decide in <2 s.

Vanish rhythm: break line of sight in 2 moves; change height or axis; dump heat.

E) Micro-beats: CQB entry (single door, hostile unknown)

Pre-set: wedge ready, mirror check, light at 30%.

Breach: hinge pop or latch lift; shoulder sliver.

Slice: step-out 15–30°, muzzle low-ready lifting with eyes.

Clear near-corner → far-corner → dead space.

ID friend/foe; act per decision tree; secure hands first.

Signal “in” with two taps on frame; pull the room to cold.

F) Micro-beats: Hallway run (two doors, one T-junction)

Hug cover; pie door one.

Shoulder flash to draw aim; re-peek opposite.

Move past door on the far wall side; hand checks latch as you pass.

At T, knee dip, slice left 30°, then right; commit only after auditory confirm.

Mark cleared doors with minimal tape glyph; no written text.
