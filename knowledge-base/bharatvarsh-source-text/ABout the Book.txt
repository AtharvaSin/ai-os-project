Book Blurb (Back Cover) — 170–190 words (Recommended)

In MahaBharatvarsh, harmony is not a promise—it’s a system.

A spotless, unified nation runs on surveillance-grade efficiency: vertical gardens masking concrete control, censored AR wrist-phones, and a military order that claims it ended chaos for good. Then, in a single coordinated strike, bombings rip through major cities—and the attackers invoke a name the state insists is long dead: Tribhuj.

Major Kahaan Arshad, a decorated officer built for precision and obedience, is assigned to lead the investigation. What begins as a manhunt turns into an audit of the nation itself. Evidence doesn’t just point to enemies outside the walls—it suggests rot within the very institution that defines “peace.”

As Kahaan follows the trail through classified labs, propaganda corridors, and engineered loyalties, every answer forces a more dangerous question: How much freedom must be sacrificed to keep a country stable—and who gets to decide the price?

MahaBharatvarsh is a political techno-thriller about control, truth, and the moment a perfect nation starts bleeding.

Book Description (Amazon / Website) — 90–120 words

A high-tech military regime has turned MahaBharatvarsh into a model of order—until coordinated bombings shatter the illusion. When the attackers resurrect the name “Tribhuj,” Major Kahaan Arshad is tasked to hunt them down. But the deeper he investigates, the more the evidence implicates the state itself: its secrets, its laboratories, and its manufactured version of peace. Caught between duty and conscience, Kahaan must choose whether to protect the system that raised him—or expose the truth and risk tearing the nation apart.

A perfect nation. Seven bombings. And an investigation that proves harmony can be engineered—at the cost of freedom.
