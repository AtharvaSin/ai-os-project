Placement logic: treat it like a brand, not a faith mark. Surfaces: wrist slates, drone casings, jammer shells, studio overlays, alley posters. Avoid placing on skin or as a ritual tattoo.

Phase 4 — Ops & Tradecraft Bible

1) Operating Doctrine (one screen for the whole crew)

Cover > Speed > Control. Success is measured by untraceable execution, not by theatrics (theatrics are assigned strategically).

Two-Minute Rule. Every intervention (jammer, breach, on-air insertion) is designed to buy 120–240 seconds; team choreography is built around those windows.

Civilian Camouflage. Overcoats, staffer fits, and exec silhouettes are chosen to read as ordinary in CCTV at a glance.

Brand as Blade. The impostor trident is a fear brand, not a faith mark; use it strategically (broadcast overlays, stickers on devices, safehouse signage) and keep it visually consistent: maroon/navy by day → neon-pink at threshold.

No fixed tells. Tool finishes, sticker placements, and carry positions rotate per mission to avoid patterning; only device families are consistent.
