Northern Plains DNA (what it must feel like)

Read at a glance: vast, geometric agriculture; orderly grids; spotless 4×4-lane expressways; polite-but-ever-present state optics. This is the bloc where “obedience feels normal.” 

Surveillance texture: random road checkpoints at algorithmic intervals; AI traffic towers; plate + passenger scanning; hovercams when crowds form. 

Green-tech veneer: highways lined with Oxy-Poles (photosynthetic air towers). 

Language discipline: Hindi primary on public signage with English sublines; regional languages persist locally but not on state highway furniture. 

Uniform law (for patrols on these sets): right shoulder = TEXT ONLY (bloc/command), e.g., “NORTHERN PLAINS COMMAND.” Never an emblem there. 

Spatial & Infrastructure Locks (your choices, productionised)

1) Highways & Control Stack

Carriageway: 4 lanes per direction (8 total) with a vegetated median (3–5 m typical).

Oxy-Poles: one every 200 m along the median; electrical poles every 50 m in between (offset to avoid visual clutter).

AI Traffic Towers: slim pylons with camera/lidar clusters every 3 km on expressways; their presence is a known canon element (AI towers). 

Night rule: no default curfew on the Plains; after dark the highway lighting drops to guidance studs only, with Oxy-Poles on low glow and towers in standby (beacons at hazard sites only).

Highway patrol cadence: every ~15 km near towns, ~50 km across empty inter-city spans. Mix of hover-sedans and bikes (uniforms follow right-shoulder text law).

Algorithmic checkpoint hardware: arch scanner + retractable bollards (median-deployed), capable of selectively “popping” a lane; randomised scheduling matches canon’s “algorithmic intervals.”

The road didn’t argue; eight lanes slid forward with the patience of a plough. Oxy-Poles rose from the median at a soldier’s cadence, greening the air; between them the lean rhythm of power masts kept time. Expressway — Night (Studs)
After dark the highway spoke in dots. Studs stitched the lanes into quiet sentences while the Oxy-Poles breathed a soft white. An AI tower waited on the horizon like a lighthouse that had learned restraint.

Service-Stop — Civic Theatre
They asked for a hand before they asked for a wallet. The pledge plinth hummed under the palm, the credo rising in Hindi with an English echo. A child tugged a sleeve toward the school exhibit where a plastic crop and a small drone performed their tidy ballet. The memorial wall read the names without asking for applause.

Checkpoint — Random Pop
No siren, no bark, just a change in the light. The arch softened through green to amber and the bollards rose as if remembering their height. The hover-bike drifted in; a pane at the wrist blinked once. Proceed, please—or hold, if the system insisted.

Township Edge — Prosperity Read
Fields resolved into drip grids as the road met houses the color of cloud. Sun-louvers blinked; roofs wore gardens and PV like practical hats. At the clinic, an elder’s wrist met a glass plate and the door answered in Hindi before repeating itself softly in English.
