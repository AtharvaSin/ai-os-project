Locked. Rolling into Street-Level Surveillance & Crowd Control — Sprint 1 (Set Bible v1) using your decisions to close the gaps. This is the production seed for boards, props, and prompt kits in later sprints.

0) Intent & Tone

Polite inevitability. Streets read orderly, bright, and safe; control tech is ever-present but restrained. Visual grammar favors monochrome marks, tri-lingual signage, clean glass & matte metal, and UI that’s translucent and illegible. Patrol optics are mixed: unobtrusive plain-clothes with armbands plus a small number of uniformed officers (right-shoulder TEXT-ONLY block, no emblem).

1) Policy Locks (on-camera rules)

Curfew: 21:00–06:00 within metropoles.

Detention threshold (escalation from spot-fine → detain & route):

Repeat violation (≥2 documented infractions in 30 days),

Curfew breach,

Flagged identity (watchlist/active alert).

Data custody: Wrist-scan/hovercam/checkpoint logs are publicly retained for 30 days, then supposedly destroyed; private archival persists beyond 30 days (implied, not posted).

Exemptions (curfew): Medical & emergency personnel, critical logistics/utility crews, accredited press, registered night-shift workers (employer QR), diplomats, and foreign tourists holding a curfew QR pass.

Audio policy: All high-decibel signals are banned in public control use. Default is soft chimes; voice PA is one-way and kept low (values in Sprint 2).

Color states: Green (pass) / Amber (additional scan) / Red (hold).

Non-lethal tools permitted (defined for props later; avoid riot optics).

2) Street Typologies & Checkpoint Layouts

We standardize three site scales so every scene can be blocked consistently.

A) Arterial Boulevard Checkpoint (Permanent Hybrid)

Placement: Every 1.2–1.6 km on major boulevards and at metro mouths.

Lanes (per carriageway): 3 lanes each side → 1 Pedestrian, 2 Automotive (cars/two-wheelers merge and are separated by light logic, not hard barriers).

Footprint: 24–30 m long active zone; 10–12 m visual taper each side.

Hardware mix: Curb-integrated scanner halos + ceiling/soffit wrist-scan strips at pedestrian lane; oxy-poles carry PA nodes + camera nests + ambient sensors; bolt-down sockets accept mobile tripod nodes during surges.

Officer presence: 1–2 plain-clothes with armbands (floating), 2 uniformed patrol at the edges (visibility, not obstruction).

B) Plaza / Festival Surge Node (Modular)

Placement: Festival corridors, parade starts, stadium/riverfront entries.

Footprint: 18–24 m modular span; mobile tripod halos with backpack power; temporary crowd rails (low, geometric, anti-trip).

Overhead: Hovercams at 8–12 m AGL (festival band) with soft searchlight etiquette for lost-child, medical egress, and dispersal.

C) Side-Street Micro Gate (Pop-up)

Placement: Alley cut-throughs, school fronts, hospital perimeters.

Footprint: 8–12 m; one Pedestrian micro-lane + one Automotive micro-lane; single oxy-pole upgraded to carry scanner head, PA, and camera nest.

Behavior: Defaults to Green-bias to keep neighborhoods flowing; flips Amber when density rises or flagged identity pings.

3) Flow Logic (Green / Amber / Red)

Input signals: wrist-scan results, plate/QR reads, hovercam density feed, watchlist pings.

Green (Pass):

Automotive: speed-gated drift (≤ 20 kph) through green chevrons; overhead pod performs silent pass-scan.

Pedestrian: walk-through arch with ceiling strip idle cyan; unobtrusive wrist-scan only on random sample (≤10%).

Amber (Additional Scan):

Automotive: gentle arrow LED guides to a halo bay; driver window QR or wrist; ≤30 s dwell.

Pedestrian: wall inset at 1.2 m/1.6 m ambidextrous; operator glance from roving plain-clothes.

Red (Hold):

Lane light flips red; low modular rail extends (motorized or manual) to shape a bay; second officer steps in; detention causes limited to: repeat, curfew breach, flagged ID.

Fail-safe: On equipment fault, system falls back to Green + manual spot-check only.

4) Hardware & Furniture (Kit of Parts)

All finishes: matte graphite anodize or low-iron glass with frosted bands; avoid chrome.

Scanner Halo — Fixed: Curb-integrated half-ring (pedestrian) and upright hoop (automotive bay). Back-light swaps Green/Amber/Red; camera pod nested at 2.2 m.

Scanner Halo — Mobile Tripod: 2.0–2.4 m telescopic mast; battery backpack dock; quick-plug to bolt-down sockets in pavement.

Wrist-Scan Modules:

Ceiling strip over thresholds (idle cyan → scan white),

Oxy-Pole Upgrade: Tri-mount bracketing for: PA node (chime + low voice), camera nest, environmental sensor; hidden conduits feed curb halos.

Candy-Bot (Child Guidance Unit): Knee-high wheeled puck with soft LED “eyes”; pastel status ring; routes children to scanner pads during festivals.

Consent: Parent taps wrist or QR at bot;

Reward: issues candy tokens (physical chips or tappable voucher) via the pad kiosk.

Modular Barriers: 1.1 m high, anti-trip base, stackable; geometry stays quiet and civic (no spikes, no chain-link).

Exemption Badges & Passes:

5) Signage, Marks & Typography (public-facing)

Tri-lingual layout: Latin (top) / Devanagari (middle) / State line (bottom).

Families: Condensed geometric sans (Latin), square-terminal grotesk (Devanagari), angular-join regional; equal stroke weight.

Curfew Signs: “CURFEW 21:00–06:00” + exemptions pictos on secondary line.

Spot-Fine Plaques: “INSTANT SPOT-FINES VIA WRIST-SCAN — CCTV ACTIVE — DATA PUBLIC 30D”.

Checkpoint Codes: GREEN PASS / AMBER SCAN / RED HOLD band strips along lane heads; arrows at 30°.

Accessibility: Wheelchair & white-cane icons co-equal in size/weight to enforcement marks.

Uniform Law: Right shoulder TEXT-ONLY (e.g., DIRECTORATE HQ or local precinct), no emblem; emblem may appear left shoulder/back.

6) Soundscape & PA Etiquette (visualized, values detailed in Sprint 2)

Default: Chime > Voice; melody cues direction or state change (Green→Amber).

Voice PA: One-way from CCTV/oxy-poles; brief, neutral, never hectoring.

Bans: Sirens, air-horns, whistles, crowd-piercing alarms.

Emergency carve-out: Medical evacuation vehicles may exceed limits transiently (documented).

7) Data & Privacy Optics (posted + implied)

Posted panel (public): “Data retention: 30 days public → purge” + appeal QR.

Implied (camera read only): Archival persistence beyond 30 days within private repositories (never written on signs; alluded via restricted-access doors, operator UI light grammar).

CCTV suite: Glassed booth visible from concourses; custody clock shows days-to-purge (counts down from 30).

10) Scene Beats to Remember (writing & boarding)

Spot-fine moment is quiet: a tap, a micro-tile glow, a white→cyan tick; no confrontation.

Dispersal is light-led: hovercam soft spotlight + chimes, not siren shoves.

Candy-bot is opt-in and friendly: LED “eyes” track only when consented; reward token dispensed at the pad.

CCTV voice is measured and rare; most guidance is visual (arrows, chevrons, LEDs).

Locked. Moving into Street-Level Surveillance & Crowd Control — Sprint 2 (Light, Materials, Signage v2). This pins concrete CCT/lux/dBA values, materials, finishes, typography, iconography, and state logic so boards, props, and prompts remain consistent across artists and vendors.

1) Lighting Look-Dev (states, levels, lenses)

Global rules

UI is translucent + illegible (steel-cyan → grey). One Brace-Comm tile at a time; quick fade; never wallpaper a frame.

Lane state colors are light, not paint: Green / Amber / Red come from low-glare emitters—never reflective vinyl.

Presets (targets; tweak per shot)

Day

CCT: 5200–5600 K.

Ambient street: 8,000–20,000 lux (sunny range); boards paint at ~2,500–4,000 lux.

Lane beacons & halos: ≤ 220 cd/m² (diffuse).

Ped arch ceiling strip: idle cyan 40%, scan white 80%.

Blue-Hour (Festival/Surge)

CCT: sky 9000–10000 K; street keys ~4200–4600 K.

Wet paving reflections encouraged (thin gloss pass).

Lane beacons & halos: 260–320 cd/m² so color states read over crowd lights.

Hovercam downlight: soft-edge 10–30 lux on subjects (no face-dazzle).

EMP-Safe / Low-Power

CCT: 3000–3200 K (amber egress strips only).

Handheld wrist readers: 120–150 lux mini-ring on contact zone.

Checkpoint field: drop to silhouettes; wayfinding stays legible via strips and arrows.

Lens cues (for boards/prompts)

Checkpoint day: 35–50 mm hero along Ped lane arch; 70–85 mm operator cut-in.

Festival surge: 21:9 at 35 mm across tripod halos; hovercam halo visible at ~10 m AGL.

Curfew night: 50 mm parallel to curb LEDs + banner glow; shallow DOF on spot-fine wrist.

2) Acoustic & PA Spec (decibel policy, tone set)

Ban line (public control use): any signal > 78 dBA (1 m) sustained or > 82 dBA peak.
Default hierarchy: Chime → Voice (never sirens/whistles/air-horns).

Chime cues: 54–60 dBA @1 m, 500–2000 Hz content, ≤ 1.5 s; 3-note rising = Amber, 1-note soft = Green resume, 2-note down = Red hold.

Voice PA (one-way): −15 LUFS target, 60–65 dBA @1 m; limited to 5-second bursts; neutral register.

Emergency carve-out: medical vehicles may exceed briefly; hovercam spotlight may pair with a single 2-note chime every 10 s during dispersal.

3) Materials & Durability (swatches, finishes, ratings)

Finish language across all furniture: matte graphite anodize + low-iron glass with frosted bands; zero chrome.

Color chips (for consistency)

Mono-Black (text/icons): #0E0F12

Mid-Grey (UI glyphs): #7C8793 @ 40–60%

Frost White (glass glow): #F4F6F8 @ 80–90%

Cyan (system): #73D5E6 @ 35–55%

Green (pass): #73E69D @ 70%

Amber (scan): #F2C14E @ 70%

Red (hold): #E05658 @ 70%

Safety Yellow (chevrons/edges): #FFD34D @ 90%

Saturations are ceilings; boards should bias toward desaturated, institutional reads.

4) Hardware Implementation (street kit: photometrics + behavior)

Scanner Halo — Fixed (Ped / Auto)

Mount heights: Ped 2.2 m soffit; Auto 1.1 m curb hoop.

Photometric: 180° forward throw, ≤ 220 cd/m² day, ≤ 320 cd/m² blue-hour.

State swap: 300 ms crossfade between Green/Amber/Red; never hard strobe.

Scanner Halo — Mobile Tripod (Festival)

Mast: 2.0–2.4 m; collapses to 1.1 m.

Power: 48 V backpack; 8 h at 50% output; hot-swap.

Sockets: plaza bolts at 3 m centers; flush caps when not in use.

Wrist-Scan Modules

Ceiling strip: 300 mm fascia; idle cyan 40%; on-scan white 80%; auto-dim after 2 s.

Wall insets: paired left/right at 1.2 m and 1.6 m heights (ambidextrous); 600 mm apart center-to-center; haptic tick on success.

Oxy-Pole Upgrade

Tri-mount: PA node (behind perforation), camera nest (gimbal micro-pod), environment sensor (PM/o³/weather).

Optical discipline: nothing protrudes > 140 mm; silhouette stays civic.

Hovercam Bands & Spotlight

Altitude: 8–12 m AGL (festival), 12–20 m AGL (non-festival), 6 m AGL (emergency drop).

Beam: 20–25° soft edge; 10–30 lux vertical on subjects; sweep ≤ 5 s per pass.

Use cases: lost-child, medical egress, dispersal (allowed).

Candy-Bot (Knee-High Puck)

Height: 420 mm; Eyes: soft LED ovals (≤ 80 cd/m²).

Speed: ≤ 1.2 m/s on plazas; obstacle stop at 300 mm.

Consent flow: parent wrist or QR → bot blinks cyan twice → child routes to scanner pad.

Reward: candy token (physical chip or tappable voucher) dispensed at pad; bot never dispenses directly.

Modular Barrier Kit

Heights/lengths: 600/900/1100 mm; 1.0 m and 1.5 m segments.

Base: anti-trip ellipse; hidden mass pocket.

Link: magnetic bump + mechanical tongue; stacks flat.

5) Signage & Wayfinding (tri-lingual, sizes, leading, tracking)

Type families (style, not brand names)

Latin (primary): condensed geometric sans; tall x-height; tabular numerals.

Devanagari (secondary): square-terminal grotesk; matches Latin cap weight.

Regional (tertiary): angular joins, open counters; weight-matched to Devanagari.

Hierarchy & letter sizes (legibility targets)

Rule of thumb: 1 mm cap height ≈ 30 mm viewing distance.

Curfew banners (over-street): Latin 180 mm / Dev 155 mm / Regional 130 mm (legible at ~5–6 m).

Lane headers (arch/halo): Latin 90 mm / Dev 78 mm / Regional 68 mm.

Plaques (data/spot-fine): Latin 40 mm / Dev 34 mm / Regional 30 mm.

Tracking/leading

Latin: +25 tracking for banners, +10 for plaques.

Devanagari & Regional: +40 (banners), +20 (plaques).

Leading: 1.15× for stacked tri-lingual blocks.

Canonical strings (tri-lingual stacked)

CURFEW 21:00–06:00 (exemptions pictos inline).

GREEN PASS / AMBER SCAN / RED HOLD (band strips).

INSTANT SPOT-FINES VIA WRIST-SCAN — CCTV ACTIVE — DATA PUBLIC 30D.

ACCESSIBILITY LANES (wheelchair + white-cane pictos).

Icon grammar

Arrows: 30° chevrons; stroke = text weight +10%.

Pictos: monochrome outlines; no skeuomorphs; accessibility icons equal weight/size to enforcement icons.

Emblems & uniforms: right shoulder TEXT-ONLY (e.g., DIRECTORATE HQ or precinct); emblem never on right.

7) Accessibility (baked into layouts)

8) Flow Diagrams (operator logic; for storyboards)

Green → Amber triggers

Randomized audit (≤ 10% ped), density > threshold, incomplete QR, partial plate read, minor confidence drop.
Amber → Red triggers

Fail-back

Loss of compute/comms: default Green with manual spot checks only + PA announces reduced service in tri-lingual text band.

Sprint 4 — Final Production Pack (Street Surveillance & Crowd Control)

0) Canon recap (locked from S1–S3)

What’s being policed: curfew breaches, repeat violations, flagged identities (hot list).

Data policy: incident logs visible to the public for 30 days, then “destroyed” (in practice, archived to private vaults).

Corridor geometry: arterial boulevards = 3 lanes each side → Lane A (pedestrian) curbside; Lanes B/C (automotive) inner/center; raised curb and bollard line separates A from B/C.

Enforcer mix: blend of plain-clothes officers and uniformed patrols.

Acoustic law: any sound above the posted dB cap is illegal (active monitoring).

Signal states: color grammar (GREEN → AMBER → RED) governs kiosks, hovercams, bollards, and wall glyphs.

Candy-bots: allowed LED “eyes”, give out candy tokens as reward (kids compliance funnel).

Crowd limits: hard density / dwell-time caps per block (enforced by hovercams/kiosks); drones also authorized for dispersal.

Exemptions: emergency medical, critical utilities, accredited press embeds, escorted dignitary convoys (all tagged in-band).

Public address: live audio, one-way (citizens cannot talk back on the PA).

No-go additions: none—no new hard bans beyond the above.

2) System Behaviors & Exact Numbers (final)

A. Sound-limit enforcement

Street cap: 75 dB(A) at 1 m in curfew windows; 85 dB(A) during festival hours.

Hovercams & pole mics triangulate; AMBER soft warning at 90% of limit; RED spot-fine at breach.

Repeat within 10 minutes → mute zone (directional dampeners) over the violator’s bubble; log increments to “repeat” tier.

B. Density & dwell

Max standing density: 3.5 persons/m² (festival), 2.0 persons/m² (normal).

Dwell cap at vendor ring: 8 minutes per ID during festival; 3 minutes on ordinary days at choke points.

Exceed → AMBER nudge on wrist + kiosk arrow to move; second exceed in 30 min → RED fine + relocation prompt.

C. Lane logic (per side)

Lane A (Ped): 2.4–3.0 m wide, textured pavers, embedded light-strips with GREEN/AMBER/RED ticks every 5 m.

Lane B (Auto-flow): glide-car stream; no stop except flagged pull-outs.

Lane C (Auto-service): kerbside service + authorized stops; quick-scan bollards at pull-offs.

D. Hovercam status (LED halo + “eye”)

Green: passive patrol; audio off; LED eyes dim.

Amber: observation focus; local PA primed; slow orbit at 6–8 m AGL.

Red: enforcement; spotlight cone tightens; video to chain-of-custody; PA live.

E. Checkpoint ladder (per individual)

First breach: verbal warning (PA), on-wrist nudge, no fine.

Second (7-day window): ₹250 spot-fine; ID flagged “observed.”

Third: ₹1000 fine + 2-hr movement restriction on the grid (soft-gates redirect).

Fourth: detain to curb-pen in Lane C; supervisor review.

F. Candy-bot protocol (minors)

Safe look: rounded cube, LED eyes (soft white/blue), mouth slot for token.

Flow: “Scan → small task → token.” Tasks = step onto pad, hold still, follow line for 3 seconds.

Reward: Candy tokens redeemable at marked kiosks (sugar or sugar-free).

Optics: always supervised by overhead hovercam (eye and bot share the same color state).

Hard rule: no identity enrollment beyond thumb; guardians can opt-out at kiosks.

G. Dispersal

Tooling: air-curtain pulses (non-injurious), fine water mist, dye-mist (expirable, 48 hrs), directional sound sweeps max 88 dB(A).

Trigger: RED density + rising dB + ignition signature (fireworks) or thrown-object vector.

H. Exemptions (visual tags)

MED-EX: medical transit (white chevron on powder-blue badge).

UTIL-EX: utilities/repairs (amber wrench glyph).

PRESS-EMB: accredited press embed (navy plate + qr-glyph).

DIGN-ESC: escorted dignitary (state seal strip + time window).

I. Transparency board (public veneer)

District wall displays: last 30 days incidents aggregate (heatmap + counts).

Small print: “For individual record access, scan here.” (Link gates to redacted public view.)

Back-end reality: full fidelity retained in private vaults (not advertised).

8) Edge-Case Plays (so storyboards don’t stall)

Repeat violator + flagged identity during festival: hovercam goes RED, pathing arrow opens to a side pen in Lane C, candy-bots shut off within a 5 m bubble, kiosk refuses tokens to linked guardians; PA switches to short, calm phrasing (“Please comply. Lane C.”).

Press exemption at dispersal: PRESS-EMB badge keeps GREEN halo, drones track but do not mist inside a 2 m press bubble; live PA continues one-way over them.
